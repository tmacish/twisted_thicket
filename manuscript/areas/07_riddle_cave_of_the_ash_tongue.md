# Area 7: Riddle Cave of the Ash Tongue

> The cave mouth is little more than a split in a rocky outcrop, black around the edges as if some ancient fire once breathed from it and left a permanent impression of itself in the stone. It is not on the gnome bark map from the north gate, the gnome knew it was here and chose not to mark it. Three openings in the outcrop face; two smell of animal and damp earth; one smells of old smoke, cold rock, something underneath that has no good name, something that has been here since before the forest had its current shape, and faintly beneath all of it, wax burned within the last day. That third opening goes deeper than it should, and something inside it has been burning a light recently enough that the wax is not yet cold.

---

## Finding the Cave

The Riddle Cave does not appear on the north gate gnome's bark map. It is a discoverable location, the bark map's absence is itself a data point, the gnome's deliberate omission of a place she considered dangerous to mark.

The cave can be found through the following:

**Nib's guidance (Area 6).** The most direct route. Nib knows exactly where the cave is and can lead the party to it in one exploration turn without any navigation roll. She calls it *the face-cave* and will not enter it, but she will wait outside.

**The knotted cord (Area 6).** The cord the scavengers found and Nib can translate reads *the cave is still safe*, and the cord is oriented so that the knots point northeast, toward the outcrop. Following the direction from the cord's orientation brings the party within fifty feet of the correct opening.

**The note from Area 6.** "Find the cave and follow the markers." The markers inside are the means of navigation; finding the cave is implicit. A party that has the note and looks northeast from the game trail junction can make a Wisdom check to notice the rocky outcrop through the treeline. Failing this check means another exploration turn of searching before the outcrop is visible.

**Three openings, one correct.** The outcrop has three cave mouths. The leftmost and rightmost smell of animal occupation, fox on the left, something larger on the right (a bear, by scent; it is not currently home). The centre opening is different: the stone around it is darker, as if old smoke stained it from inside rather than outside, and the smell is cold ash and something mineral that has no forest equivalent. A Ranger identifies the centre opening without a roll. Any other character who spends one round examining all three makes the same determination on a successful Wisdom check.

---

## The Entry Passage

The centre opening narrows immediately into a passage eight feet long and barely two feet wide. A Medium character must turn sideways and move carefully, one round to pass through, no roll required, but a character in plate mail or carrying a large shield must leave it at the entrance or make a Strength check to squeeze through (on a failed check, the character is stuck for one round and makes noise). A Large creature cannot enter at all.

Beyond the passage, the cave opens. The first party member to clear the passage can see, before they have fully entered: candlelight. Steady, nearly spent, at the far wall.

---

## The Outer Chamber

Twenty feet across, twelve feet high at the apex, the chamber is roughly oval with a level floor of compressed earth and limestone dust. The walls are covered:

**The old symbols.** On the lower six feet of every wall surface, scratched rather than carved, in a hand that predates any current civilisation in the Shield Lands by centuries: repeating geometric patterns, interlocking spirals, and among them a recurring image of a face, the same face as the carved stone to the north, rendered dozens of times in a style that is consistent across all instances. This face was here before the cave had any other purpose. Whatever it is, it was important to whoever made it, and they wanted it seen from every angle in the room.

**The soot marks.** Higher on the walls, in irregular patches: fire residue from torches and small cookfires. Multiple layers, multiple eras. The oldest soot is deep brown with age; the newest is still properly black and was left recently. Old Brindle, if present, can date the layers roughly: the oldest are decades old, the newest within the week.

**The berry-red handprints.** At roughly gnome height on the east wall: a cluster of small handprints pressed in plumeberry dye. Six distinct hands, probably from six different gnomes. The prints are not recent, a month, perhaps two, but they are not the oldest marks in the room. This cave has been a gnome waystation for some time. Nib, if she has come inside despite her stated preference, will look at the handprints for a long moment without speaking.

**The trappers' corner.** South wall, floor level: the debris of the cave's more recent mundane use. A rusted canteen, a rotted bedroll frame, a broken leather strap that was once part of a pack. Rumour #14 was true, this was a waystation before the current trouble started. The trappers are gone now. The debris is theirs.

**The figure at the north wall.** The first character through the entry passage enters the outer chamber and stops.

Seated against the north wall, near the carved stone face, is a person. Dark wool and green silk. Narrow boots, soles matching the profile of the tracks from Area 6. A nearly spent candle on a flat stone beside them. A spanned crossbow on the stone with a bolt in the groove, angled toward the passage opening. One hand rests near the crossbow. The other is open, visible, and still.

They are not asleep. They heard the party long before the first character cleared the passage. They have had time to do whatever they intended to do about that, and what they chose was to wait.

When the first party member enters, they look at them. Then, if a weapon is visible, they look at the weapon. Then they look at the party member's face.

They do not speak first.

---

## Varek Sorn

The figure at the north wall is the silk-wearing operative who preceded the party through the Thicket. They killed Kael Vorst at Area 3 and Orn at Area 6. They solved the puzzle here, opened the cache, and did not continue north. They chose to wait. They want to see who followed the weapons this far into the forest.

**AC** -2 (bracers of defense, Dexterity, ring of protection +1); **Class** Thief 12 / Mage 14; **hp** 55; **MV** 120 ft.  
**THAC0** 11 (+2 short sword); **Attacks** +2 short sword (paralytic poison) or dagger (thrown, 30 ft./60 ft./120 ft.)  
**Backstab** x4 damage when attacking from concealment or surprise  
**Str** 12, **Int** 18, **Wis** 14, **Dex** 18, **Con** 13, **Cha** 15  
**Saving Throws** Death/Poison 8, Wands 4, Paralyzation 8, Breath 10, Spells 7  
**Thief Skills** Move Silently 97%, Hide in Shadows 95%, Find/Remove Traps 90%, Open Locks 99%, Hear Noise 55%  
**Spells per day** 1st: 5, 2nd: 5, 3rd: 5, 4th: 4, 5th: 4, 6th: 3, 7th: 2

**Poison.** The short sword carries a paralytic venom. A creature struck must save vs. Death/Poison or be paralyzed for 1d4 turns. She is not trying to kill the party. She is removing threats from the board.

**Pre-cast protections.** Before the party arrived she had already cast Stoneskin (absorbs the first 1d4+8 physical hits entirely; roll privately and note the result before the session) and Mirror Image (8 images; each successful hit against her destroys one image instead of harming her). Both are in effect when the party enters. A party that has not encountered high-level magic will spend several rounds learning why their weapons are not working. She does not explain. She watches them figure it out.

**The Contingency.** She carries a pre-set Contingency spell, trigger condition: if reduced to 10 hp or fewer, Teleport Without Error fires instantly, no action required, no saving throw, no warning. She is gone. Not fleeing, not retreating: simply absent, mid-combat if necessary, between one round and the next. No sound, no flash. The cave is empty where she was. The party cannot prevent it. They cannot follow. They cannot claim a kill. She chose her destination before she entered the Thicket and it is not anywhere in the Shield Lands the party currently knows.

The referee should hold this as a design fact: Varek Sorn cannot be killed in this adventure. She can be dealt with, fought until the Contingency fires, or left behind. But she will never leave a body. She always exits on her own terms. That is fifty years of operational practice made into a rule.

---

### The Name

In certain guild circles and counting-rooms, the name Sarra of the Keys carries weight, and it is spoken quietly. Whether this is Varek Sorn's true name, an operational alias, or a legend they have borrowed is not established in this adventure. The party may draw their own conclusions.

The legend, as it is told: she does not move ordinary cargo. She moves the things that open other things. Sealed correspondence across closed borders. Documents that unlock succession lines. Route intelligence that opens passage into places people are not supposed to enter. She has been doing this for thirty years across the Flanaess, and she has never been caught and never, to any guild's knowledge, left a witness who could identify her to someone who would use that information against her.

The legend ends the same way in every version. Someone who knows the name asks what she has not moved yet. The answer, among those who know enough to answer: the locked things under the smoking mountain. Those, they say, are next.

**Recognition.** A character with guild connections, thieves' guild membership, or significant experience in Critwall's merchant circles may make an Intelligence check. On a success, they recognize the operational profile: the precision kills, the silk, the narrow sole, the evident ability to move through hostile terrain at night without detection, and the scope of what has been routed through this forest. They have heard the name without knowing the face. The name, if the check succeeds, is Sarra of the Keys.

If the character says the name aloud in the outer chamber, she pauses, one beat, no more. *"That name is older than the person using it. I find it useful that people still know it."* She does not confirm or deny.

---

### The Encounter

She does not initiate combat. She is assessing: who are these people, what did they find, and what do they intend to do with it. She is prepared for the possibility that the answer is an attack.

**What she knows.** She has been in the Thicket for several days. She knows about the wagon crash. She knows the weapons were separated from the convoy. She has been feeling their movement since they left Coach B, and the weapons do not travel without being felt by someone attentive. If the party is carrying Wave, Whelm, or Blackrazor, she knows within one round of them entering the chamber.

If the party is carrying all three: she is quiet for a moment before she speaks. That was not in the plan.

**She does not speak first.** She waits to see what the party does.

**Opening lines.** If the party asks who she is: *"That depends on what you've heard."* If the party demands answers without offering them first: she says nothing and lets the silence do its work. If the party shows a weapon openly: *"You found them. Both, or all three?"* If the party attacks without words: she acts on initiative.

---

### Outcomes

**The deal.** She wants the weapons to go north. She has spent years making sure they would. If the party is going north, she has no reason to oppose them and several reasons to ensure they arrive. She can offer: the puzzle solution (she has already solved it and will confirm the steps plainly, not demonstrate), confirmation that a silk thread in the cache recess is still armed and how to disarm it without triggering the dust signal, and a verbal briefing on the bark map's most critical landmarks. In exchange she asks for one thing: do not name her to Elnara or to anyone connected to Sorel Vane's operation. She is not threatening. She simply does not want to be found by the people who are currently looking for her.

She will not say what she intends when she reaches the mountain. She will not confirm her name. If pressed about the Revocation, she stops talking.

She will say, if the deal is made and the party seems capable: *"The mountain is not a destination. It is a door. Someone needs to be on the other side of it with the right things in their hands. That is why the weapons matter. That is why they had to come this way."* She will not say what is on the other side.

**The fight.** If combat begins, she acts on initiative. The first character through the entry passage who has not yet had time to orient is in the backstab position: the passage forces characters to emerge one at a time, and she has been sitting at the correct angle for hours. She poisons the largest physical threat first.

Her Stoneskin absorbs the first several hits without damage and her Mirror Images consume the rest one by one. A party that has not encountered high-level magical protection will spend rounds attempting to understand why their weapons are not working. She does not explain. She watches. When they stop swinging and start thinking is when the conversation she wanted can actually happen.

She does not fight to the death because the Contingency makes that impossible. If her defenses are stripped and the party lands significant damage, she drops to 10 hp and she is simply gone, mid-round, between one action and the next. If she chooses to leave before the Contingency becomes relevant, Dimension Door (4th-level, instant, no saving throw) drops her 360 feet away in the treeline before anyone can act on it.

**The chase.** There is no chase. She does not run; she departs. The bark map and the cache remain. The party is left in an empty cave with the unsettled knowledge that they could not stop someone they may not fully understand. Whether they meet Varek Sorn again is not a question. They will. The answer is in the mountain.

---

### What She Will and Will Not Say

She will confirm: the weapons belong at the mountain. She moved them toward it because capable hands need to carry them through what comes next. Sorel Vane was operating under direction from a figure claiming to be Keraptis who is not the historical Keraptis, and that figure has been hunting the weapons for a long time.

She will not confirm: her name. Her allegiance. What the Revocation is or does. What waits at the mountain when the weapons arrive.

**If the party presses her on personal motivation** (why does this matter to you, why have you done this for thirty years): she is quiet for one beat. Then: *"Someone I knew went into the dark before any of this started. I have been finishing what they would have done."* She does not say the name. She does not explain further. If pressed again, she changes the subject with the particular efficiency of someone who has decided they have already said too much. This is, in the context of who she is and what she has built over fifty years, as close to a confession as she will ever make to strangers.

**If the party has the Revocation** (the lead-lined case from Area 2): her manner changes the moment she sees it. Not to alarm or greed, to the particular stillness of someone in the presence of a thing that costs them something. She does not reach for it. She does not ask where they found it. After a moment she says: *"Keep that safe."* Two words. She will not say more about it. A character who watches her carefully in that moment, and who later reads the history document the party may have found in Ringstown, will understand what those two words cost her.

## The Puzzle: The Ash Tongue Face

Carved into the north wall at eye height for a standing human, roughly five feet off the floor, approximately two feet across, is a stone face. Not a relief sculpture but a mechanism: the face is stylised, almost masklike, with two elements that are movable.

**The eye.** The left eye of the face is a round river stone, polished, set into a carved socket with enough clearance to rotate. It turns freely clockwise or counterclockwise with light finger pressure. In its current neutral position, the pupil marking faces straight ahead.

**The tongue.** Protruding slightly from the face's open mouth is a tongue-shaped extension of the same pale limestone as the surrounding wall. It can be pressed inward and held; when released, it returns to its extended position.

**The riddle**, carved in an arch above the face in Old Flan runes (readable with a successful Intelligence check or by any character with knowledge of ancient languages; Old Brindle can read it, as can a gnome of the mountain lineage):

> *When white breath wears a mortal face,*
> *Seek not the height but hollow place.*
> *Turn the eye and still the tongue,*
> *There lies the path for old and young.*

**Interpreting the riddle.** "White breath wears a mortal face" refers to the plume above White Plume Mountain, the white smoke that forms a face at dusk, which the party has now seen from Area 4. "Seek not the height but hollow place", the mountain's secrets are underground, not at the summit. "Turn the eye and still the tongue", the mechanism. "There lies the path for old and young", the cache below reveals the route, accessible to anyone.

**Operating the mechanism:**
1. Turn the carved eye clockwise. The stone rotates with a faint grinding sound. After three-quarters of a turn it reaches a stop with a soft click.
2. Press the stone tongue inward and hold it.
3. Remain in complete silence for one full round. No speech, no striking of armour, no movement that produces noise. The referee should count this out, ask the table to be quiet, and count ten seconds of real silence. If any player speaks during this time (not the characters, the players), reset. If the group holds silence, the mechanism completes.

At the end of the silent round: a seam appears in the floor directly below the face, a rectangle approximately 18 inches by 12, and a stone panel slides aside revealing a shallow recess cut into the limestone floor.

**If the mechanism is forced or circumvented.** The eye and tongue are stone; they cannot be broken without significant effort (treated as a 2-inch stone wall, 4 inch equivalent with the reinforcement of the surrounding rock). Forcing the eye counterclockwise after it has been turned clockwise resets it to neutral with another grinding sound. There is no trap. Rumour #45 was wrong. The mechanism simply does not open if operated incorrectly. A party that cannot solve the riddle has a cache they cannot open.

**The obsidian method (already spent).** The note from Area 6 referenced a second entry method: pressing an intact obsidian disc against the face's left eye bypasses the turning and silence requirement, opening the panel immediately. If the party used the obsidian disc at Area 5 to sever the owlbear's control, it arrived here cracked and dead ,  testing it produces nothing. If the disc was never used at Area 5, it is intact and warm, but it does not activate this mechanism; it is keyed to a different door than the one behind this face. If the party tries the disc first and fails, the riddle is still solvable; the mechanism is unharmed.

## The Cache

The recess is six inches deep. It contains:

**The bark-map.** A flat piece of birch bark, roughly 12 inches by 8, covered on both sides with notations in multiple hands. This is not a single person's work but an accumulated record: the primary route from the cave north to White Plume Mountain, with additions and corrections made by different travellers over years. The notation style shifts, one hand is gnomish, one is a trapper's shorthand, one is precise and educated, one is hasty. Distances are marked in hours of travel. Landmarks include: *the iron-red spring*, *the place where the trail forks at a dead ash* (marked *take east*), *the entry stone with the K that faces sunrise*, and finally *the vent that smells of sulphur and does not show smoke from outside*. The mountain does not appear on this map as an approach but as an entry, the map is for going in, not for finding it.

**Two ceramic dust tubes.** Sealed with beeswax. Standard white plume dust, 1 dose each. Left here by the operative or a prior occupant, unclear from physical evidence which. They are here because someone believed anyone who reached this cache might need them. The belief that the dust is useful to the people using this cave says something about those people.

**A gnome warning, written in Gnomish.** On a strip of pressed bark in small, careful handwriting: *The third vent from the east leads to the gathering chamber. The gathering chamber is not safe when the smoke is white above. Wait for the smoke to turn grey before entering from below. The gathering chamber floor is uneven and the gnomes who know the path are not always there. If you hear hammering below, do not call out, let it stop on its own.* This note was left by someone who has been inside White Plume Mountain. The warning about the smoke corresponds to the face in the smoke at dusk that the party has seen from Area 4: white smoke means the false Keraptis is active. Grey smoke means he is occupied or dormant. The hammering note is unexplained and should remain so.

**A fresh obsidian disc.** Identical in shape to Kael Vorst's disc from Area 3 (if the party recovered it), same diameter, same polish, same warmth when held. This one is whole and intact. If the party has Kael's disc ,  cracked from use at Area 5, or uncracked if they never used it there ,  examining them together confirms they are the same type of object. A party that skipped Area 3 will not have Kael's disc or his name; the disc comparison still functions if they have any intact obsidian disc from elsewhere. This disc is warm in a way that suggests it is listening rather than broadcasting. It is a key, and the lock is somewhere north, underground. It did not activate the face mechanism because it is keyed to a different door.

## The Detection Mechanism

The operative left a watcher behind.

A single thread of fine dark silk, matching the fabric torn on the hollow's thorn wall, is stretched across the inner face of the stone panel's recess at the level of the cache contents. It is nearly invisible in torchlight; a character who examines the interior of the recess before reaching in has a chance to spot it on a successful Wisdom check, or automatically if they are a Thief making a Search check. The thread connects to a small ceramic vial wedged into a crack in the stone panel's underside.

If the thread is broken (by reaching into the cache without noticing it), the vial cracks against the stone edge and releases a pinch of white plume dust into the air of the outer chamber, enough to trigger saving throws for anyone present (save vs. Death/Poison or experience one round of disorienting euphoria, no mechanical combat effect, but the party will know they triggered something). More importantly: a thin thread of glittering white powder drifts out through the entry passage and disperses into the outside air in a brief visible puff.

This signal is visible at range. The operative prepared it before opening the cache and knows it is there. If she is present when the thread is broken, she will react with quiet assessment, not alarm: she knows exactly what the dust does. In a deal outcome, she will tell the party about the thread and how to disarm it cleanly before they reach into the cache. If she has fled or been killed before the party reaches the cache, the signal functions as written. The false Keraptis is watching through any active dust in the area, and the pool at Area 4 is now either closed (if the party cleansed it) or active. **If the pool was cleansed:** the signal is simply a puff of dust that means nothing to anyone watching; the scrying connection is severed. **If the pool was not cleansed:** the false Keraptis sees it, and knows the cave has been found.

**Disabling it.** A Thief can cut the thread cleanly and remove the vial without triggering it, given one round of careful work. A careful non-Thief can attempt the same on a Dexterity check. The disarming requires both steps: cut the thread AND catch or immobilise the vial before it can crack against the stone. Cutting the thread alone ,  without first securing the vial ,  still triggers the signal, even if the cut was deliberate. Leaving the mechanism intact (carefully replacing the stone panel without breaking the thread) is also an option: it is never triggered, and whoever is watching for the signal receives no indication either way.

## Leaving the Cave

The entry passage runs the same both directions: eight feet, shoulder-width, one round to traverse. Stepping out into open air after the cave's close dark is abrupt, the smell of forest returns all at once, and the narrow angle of the cave mouth means the party exits facing northwest, into the treeline.

The mountain is to the northeast. On a clear day it is visible above the canopy from the rocky outcrop. At dusk, it is unmistakable.

**If the party exits at dusk:** Read this aloud.

> The plume above the mountain is catching the last light. As it does, for perhaps three or four seconds, no longer, the steam holds a shape. The same face. The one from the north wall, rendered in white against a copper sky: the wide brow, the flat planes, the open mouth. It does not move. It does not look toward you. It simply exists, the way it has existed at every dusk above this mountain, and then the light shifts and it is steam again.

This is not magic. The geyser exhales at a specific temperature at this hour, and the steam meets cold air from the west at the angle that produces this effect. The face has always formed at dusk. Whoever carved the mechanism chose that face because it matches what the mountain shows at sunset, not as a mystery to solve, but as an acknowledgment: *I know the same thing you know.* Whatever made both faces was paying attention.

The Northern Rise, the basalt foothills between here and the mountain's base, is visible in the fading light as a dark irregular line below the plume. The bark-map's first landmark begins somewhere out there. The distance, for the first time, feels measurable.

---

## What the Cave Knows

The cave is old. Its oldest marks predate the current political structure of the Shield Lands, possibly predate the Aerdy presence entirely. The repeated face on the walls, the one that became the mechanism, was made by someone who considered it important, repeated it obsessively, and then left. No remains, no artefacts, no explanation. The face is not Keraptis's work. It is not gnomish. It predates both by centuries and possibly millennia.

The gnomes began using the cave because it was sheltered and because they understand old things without requiring explanations for them. The trappers used it because the gnomes left it safe and they were practical people. The operative used it because the route north required a waystation and this one already existed, which meant no construction, no permanent occupation, no evidence beyond what a careful search would find.

The cave does not care about any of this. It was here before all of them and will be here after. The Ash Tongue face is the oldest thing in the Thicket that is not a tree, and unlike the trees it has not been corrupted by the dust. Whatever made it made it to last.

## Referee Notes

**On the name "Ash Tongue."** Old Brindle knows this cave by that name and will explain it if asked: the face is a very old Flan spirit-marker, representing something the Flan called the Voice of the Ash, a forest intelligence that communicated through burned wood and the patterns left by fire on stone. The tongue of the face is carved from ash-grey stone deliberately, not coincidentally. The Flan didn't build the cave, they found it and named what was already there, which is a habit of people who are paying attention. Old Brindle finds the cave's continued existence, uncorrupted, faintly reassuring. He is not a demonstrative man, so this will manifest as a slightly less exhausted expression for the rest of the evening.

**On the gnome warning.** Nib can read it. If she does, she will go quiet for a moment and then say: *"Whoever wrote this went in. They came back out. That is more than most."* She does not know who wrote it. She knows the third vent from the east, she has been near enough to the mountain to know the ventilation pattern. She has never gone inside and will not, but she knows this note is accurate. The hammering is something she refuses to explain, and the refusal is not coy. She is genuinely afraid of the answer.

**On the fresh obsidian disc.** It is a key to a specific mechanism inside White Plume Mountain, the one the cracked disc would have opened if used for cave entry had not spent it. It fits a different lock than the face mechanism. A party entering White Plume Mountain with this disc has an advantage a party without it does not: a door that requires no force, no guessing, no spell. The operative's disc ,  the one used to bypass this mechanism on entry ,  is spent. A different disc, Kael Vorst's or the party's, if brought here, is keyed to a different door entirely. The door on the other side is at the end of the route on the bark-map. A referee running White Plume Mountain as the sequel should decide what that door opens before the party arrives.

**On the dust in the cache.** The two ceramic tubes present the party with one last decision about the substance that has defined this entire adventure. They can destroy it here, carry it out to Elnara, or keep it. The adventure does not make this decision for them. A party that has watched Dort carve K-marks into stone, watched Pirrel lose herself, watched the owlbear, a creature that did nothing wrong except find a crate, tear through three counties in a state of contaminated ecstasy, may find the choice easy. Or they may not. That's the question the adventure has been asking since the session zero crash.

**On the conclusion available here.** The Riddle Cave is the adventure's furthest point of travel and its deepest information deposit. A party that completes all seven areas has:
- Recovered two of the three legendary weapons
- Identified the smuggling network's key personnel
- Discovered that the owlbear was deliberately created as a weapon and its territory deliberately prepared
- Established that someone above Sorel Vane is cleaning house and moving north
- Learned a specific route into White Plume Mountain and specific navigation intelligence for its interior

This is not the end of the story. The cave, the bark-map, the gnome's note, and the fresh obsidian disc make that clear. But this adventure ends here, at the mouth of something larger, with the party possessing more than they expected to find when Elnara said *I need it stopped, the beast, the pool, the cache, whatever is feeding this.* They stopped those things. They have also learned that someone built them, and that person is still building.

The plume above the mountain is visible from the cave mouth if the party looks north. At dusk, the face forms in the smoke. It does not look surprised to be found.

**On Varek Sorn.** The full encounter entry is in the Varek Sorn section above. The key referee note: she chose to wait at this cave rather than continue north, which is the single most important fact about her. A person who could leave and chose not to is making a statement. She wants to know who has been following the weapons through the forest. The party should feel, when they find her, that they have been expected.

Her allegiance is not established in this adventure and should not be resolved here. The available evidence supports at least three interpretations, and she will not narrow it. Let the party argue about her on the road north.

The canonical reading of this adventure is that Varek Sorn is Sarra: the woman named in the history document, Laski's younger sister, the factor from Critwall who lost the person she steered by to the thing in the dark water and then spent fifty years finishing what they would have done. The three aliases, the Critwall origin, the scope of what she has moved toward this mountain, the way she reacts to the Revocation, and the "someone I knew" admission all point the same direction. The module text keeps it ambiguous to give referees room, but the intention is that she is Sarra, and the history document was written to receive that confirmation when a referee is ready to give it.

The Laski thread is not resolved in this adventure. It should not be. The party plants the seed at Area 7. The history document waters it. The confirmation belongs in SF2, underground, when the party meets her again and she finally says the name aloud. That is the scene this adventure is building toward.
