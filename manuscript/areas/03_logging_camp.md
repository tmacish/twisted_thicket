# Area 3: Logging Camp

> The logging camp looks as if a storm developed opinions about it. Tarps hang in strips from their poles, split logs are scattered through the mud in patterns that suggest both panic and physics, and deep claw marks run up the watchtower supports from ground level to fifteen feet in a single continuous gouge. The cookfire still smolders under a pot kicked half over in the mud. Somewhere in the wreckage, someone is moving, careful, practiced movement, the kind that belongs to a person who doesn't want to be seen.

---

## Camp Layout

The camp occupies a cleared quarter-acre of forest floor, running roughly north-south, with the Thicket pressing in on three sides. The south approach is the clearest path in; the north end of the camp opens onto a trampled area of felled timber before the trees close again. Five structures remain, in varying states of intact:

**The Bunkhouse.** The camp's main sleeping structure, a long low building of rough-cut timber, took the owlbear's first impact on its northeast corner. That corner is demolished; the remaining three walls are standing but bowed. The bunks inside have been thrown from their frames. Three loggers' personal effects are scattered across the floor: a good pair of boots, a carved pipe, a letter in trade tongue that was never finished. Nothing worth taking. But the details reward a character who stops to look.

**The Watchtower.** The north end of the camp. Fifteen feet of notched timber, the upper platform intact, the two support posts on the south face gouged by the same claw sweep that tore the bunkhouse corner. The ladder rungs are intact. From the platform, the party can see the felled timber area to the north and the tree line where the owlbear exited. The view north is the clearest sight line in the area and worth the climb: two large pine trunks are knocked flat in the direction of Area 5, a trail no tracking roll is needed to read.

**The Supply Shed.** South end of camp. The padlock hangs open in the hasp, not broken, not cut, but picked clean, the tumblers visibly scored by a tool. Whoever emptied the shed did not bother to relock it on the way out; they were in a hurry or simply did not care. Inside: logging tools, lamp oil, a quarter-cask of salted provisions, and, notably, an empty space in the centre of the floor where something large was kept and recently removed. The outline in the dust is rectangular, the right size for a shipping crate. Whatever was stored here was taken before the owlbear arrived, not during or after.

**The Cookfire Station.** The pot kicked into the mud still contains a day-old stew. Two tin cups on the ground nearby. The fire was recently stoked, within the hour, which means someone was trying to stay warm, which means someone was here long enough to want comfort.

**The Felled Timber Yard.** North of the watchtower, not really a structure: a cleared area where trunks are stacked and sectioned. Two of the stacks have been knocked over. The owlbear came through here on its way south into the camp, and again on its way north leaving. The prints are enormous and easy to follow. The trail north leads directly toward Area 5.

---

## What Happened Here

The attack was mid-afternoon, not at night. The logging crew, seven men, was working the timber yard when the owlbear came south from the tree line at speed. It struck the bunkhouse, swept the north fence posts, crossed the camp in three strides, and was gone north again in under two minutes. Four of the seven loggers fled south and reached the main road. Two hid under the supply shed flooring and emerged after dark. One, Pell, the actual camp foreman, ran north in the confusion and was killed in the timber yard. His body is still there, partially obscured by a fallen trunk. He is not the most significant body in this camp.

**Kael Vorst.** Near the bunkhouse wreckage, propped against the south wall in a position suggesting he sat down and did not get up: a man in his middle years, well-fed for a logger, in clothes that are almost right for the work. His wounds are consistent with the owlbear's claw pattern. A character with healing experience (Cleric, Druid, Ranger, or a successful Intelligence check) who examines the body carefully notices an inconsistency: the deepest wound, the one that killed him, enters from the back, angled slightly downward, the profile of a blade thrust from a standing person to a kneeling one. The owlbear's claws are not blades. *Kael Vorst was stabbed in the back during the chaos and the claw wounds were added afterward, either by the beast or by whoever killed him.* The killer is gone. The instrument, a narrow blade, is not on the body or in the immediate area.

Kael Vorst is the K-contact from the Area 2 ledger. He was not a logger. He was sent here to collect the crates and move them north. He arrived two days ago posing as a replacement foreman; the real foreman, recognising something was wrong, found an excuse to be in Ringstown. Kael's body has no identification. His belt pouch contains 34 gp, a sealed vial of the standard white plume dust antitoxin (the same formula Kelpwick sells), and a small polished obsidian disc, blank on both faces, warm to the touch in a way that has nothing to do with sun or body heat.

---

## What's Here

**The obsidian disc** (on Kael's body): No inscription, no markings, warm. A Magic-User or Cleric who holds it and concentrates (one round, uninterrupted) senses faint directed intention, not a spell, not psionic, but structured. Like a key that has been near its lock. It does not appear in Area 7 (the Riddle Cave) by coincidence.

**The supply shed's emptied space:** Whatever was stored here before the owlbear arrived is not in the camp. A wax seal fragment in the dust matches Blink Tinderflip's shipping mark. The party has now found this mark in three places: Area 1 (the broken crate), Area 2 (the false-bottom crate), and here. Someone moved this consignment before the attack, possibly Kael himself, possibly whoever killed him.

**Pell's body** (in the timber yard): The real foreman. No suspicious wounds. His belt has a keyring with the supply shed key, which explains why the lock was picked rather than opened. If the party has been wondering who has the shed key, Pell does, which means whoever emptied the shed either had another key, picked the lock, or took the key from Pell before his death. The timeline is unclear.

**The owlbear trail north:** From the timber yard, two flattened pines point north like an arrow. No tracking roll is required to follow this to Area 5. Characters who follow the trail also notice, at the edge of the tree line, a cluster of fresh K-marks carved into three adjacent pines, not by the owlbear's claws (the angle is wrong, the lines too deliberate) but by a human hand with a small blade. They were not here before the attack. Someone came to the camp after the owlbear left and carved them.

---

## The Encounter: Sorel Vane and Crew

Sorel Vane arrived at the camp four hours ago with four of his people. He found the wreck, found Kael's body, and has been waiting since. He is not waiting for the crates, he knows by now they are not coming, he is waiting for something to make sense of this, which it has not yet done. One of the four, Orn, has not returned since leaving to check the treeline K-marks; see Referee Notes.

His crew is spread through the camp: one watching the south approach from behind the bunkhouse, one in the watchtower, one, Pirrel, sitting near the cookfire doing something with her belt knife that she does not appear to notice she is doing. The person the party detected moving at the start of this area is Sorel himself, circling the supply shed again.

**First contact.** Sorel is not hiding, exactly, but he is not announcing himself either. When the party enters the camp, he will let them get roughly to the cookfire before stepping out of cover and addressing them: *"You're not loggers. You're not Kael's people. So, who sent you, and what are you carrying?"* He is evaluating threat, not picking a fight. His hand is near his crossbow but not on it.

**If the party has the coded ledger from Area 2:** Sorel recognises his own shorthand immediately and his expression closes. He will ask, very quietly, what happened to Mave and Dort. He will not attack unless the party answers in a way that confirms they killed his people for the cache alone. If the party handled Mave and Dort non-lethally or explains the situation, Sorel becomes less hostile and more calculating.

**If the party mentions Blink Tinderflip:** Sorel's jaw tightens. He will not confirm or deny the name in front of his crew. He will say: *"That's a name I'd rather not say out loud in the Thicket. Come inside."* He is willing to speak privately.

**If the party shows the manifest from Area 2 naming "E. Forger":** Sorel laughs, a short, unhappy sound, and says: *"Elnara. Of course she was already moving pieces."* He has now confirmed the party's connection to Elnara, and the dynamic shifts. He is not afraid of Elnara exactly, but he respects her competence and that respect modulates his options.

**What Sorel will trade for safe passage south:**

Sorel does not want to fight the party. He wants to leave the Thicket alive and with enough distance from this operation that when it comes apart, and it is coming apart, his name is not at the centre of it. He will offer the following information freely if the party agrees not to bring his name to Elnara:

- The dust is manufactured, not harvested, there is a laboratory. He has never seen it but has been told it is underground, north of the Thicket, connected to the mountain.
- His employer above Blink Tinderflip uses the title *the Inheritor.* Sorel has never met or seen this person. All contact went through Kael. Kael is dead.
- The K-shorthand in the coded ledger is not a reference to Keraptis, it was Kael's personal mark, a professional signature. Sorel did not choose it. In retrospect, *yes, I see how that looks.*
- The emptied supply shed was Kael's doing, Kael had a separate consignment already in place when the crew arrived, and he moved it himself before the owlbear hit. Sorel does not know where it went.
- The obsidian disc, if shown to Sorel: he has seen one before. Kael wore one around his neck at their first meeting and later stopped wearing it. He does not know what they are.

**What Sorel will not trade, at any price:** The identity of the buyers in Critwall from the partial ledger in Area 2. Those are people with long memories and Sorel has a family in Critwall.

**Pirrel.** Sorel's fourth crew member, sitting at the cookfire, is carving something into the log she sits on. She has been doing this for two hours. She is carving the letter K, over and over, in a grid-like pattern, filling each previous row before starting a new one. She is not in combat distress, she will fight if the camp is attacked, and she is still effective, but her eyes are slightly wrong, white visible around the iris. If the party includes a healer or anyone who has seen advanced dust exposure, a Wisdom check recognises this as mid-stage compulsion. Sorel knows something is wrong with her; he does not know it is the dust. He thinks it's shock. He is wrong. If the party explains what is happening to her, Sorel's cooperative threshold drops significantly.

**Fighter 4**, Human, Male
**AC** 5 *(studded leather + Dex)*, **HD** 4, **hp** 27
**Attacks** 1/round: short sword +3 to hit, 1d6+1 damage; or light crossbow +4 to hit, 1d4+1 damage (range 6"/12"/18")
**Saving Throws** Death/Poison 11, Wands 12, Paralyzation 13, Breath 14, Spells 15
**Morale** 10 *(drops to 7 if two crew members are down)*
**Str** 12, **Int** 13, **Wis** 9, **Dex** 15, **Con** 11, **Cha** 14
**Equipment** Studded leather, short sword, light crossbow, 20 bolts, 22 gp, a written contact address in Critwall (encoded, inside boot lining)

**Tactics:** Sorel fights with professional economy, he identifies the most dangerous target and addresses it, uses cover instinctively, and will attempt to disengage and run south if reduced below 8 hp. He does not sacrifice himself. He will call his crew off if he decides to negotiate.

## Referee Notes

**On Kael Vorst's killer:** The party may not discover the murder during this visit; it depends on whether anyone examines the body carefully. Do not volunteer the inconsistency, reward it if found. The killer was not Sorel (he found the body; the timeline holds up under questioning). The killer came from the north, used the owlbear as cover, and left north again. This is someone with operational knowledge of both the attack's timing and the camp's role in the supply chain. The obsidian disc is the only thread, and it leads to Area 7.

**On how Orn was separated from the crew:** Among the K-marks carved into the three pines at the north treeline, the operative left a sealed note wedged into a bark split at eye level, addressed to Orn by name. The note instructed him to follow the marked trail north and collect a package that had been left for him. The fact that the operative knew Orn's name specifically, not Sorel's, not Tav's or Hessig's, suggests the Inheritor's network has detailed intelligence on Sorel's crew composition, a fact that should alarm Sorel considerably if the party tells him. The note is gone (Orn took it); only the empty bark split remains, visible to anyone who examines the K-marks closely. Sorel did not see Orn leave because Orn waited until Sorel was occupied before checking the treeline marks, he recognised the operative's signalling convention from prior jobs.

**On the emptied supply shed:** Whatever was in it is not findable in this adventure. It is a loose end pointing forward, to White Plume Mountain proper, if this adventure serves as a prologue. Leave it open. The wax seal fragment is evidence enough that Blink's network was using this location as a depot before the owlbear disrupted everything.

**On Sorel as an ongoing resource:** If the party treats Sorel fairly, takes his information, honors the safe passage agreement, maybe even gets Pirrel treated, he becomes a useful contact in Critwall. He knows the buyer network. He has no loyalty to the Inheritor, only to being alive. A referee running this as a campaign entry point should note his address in Critwall and let Sorel be a thread the party can pull later.

**On Pirrel:** If the party does not treat her and Sorel decides to head south, he will take her with him. She will get worse. By the time they reach the forest edge she will be unresponsive to anything except the compulsion. Sorel cannot make himself leave her there. He will ask the party, one more time, if there is anything that can help. This is not a trap. It is a man watching his crew come apart and not knowing why.

**On the K-marks at the tree line:** These were not made by any of Sorel's crew (Sorel will confirm this, and none of his people are missing a blade with the right profile). They were not made by the owlbear. Someone with intent came to this camp after the attack and marked the trail north as though directing something, or someone, to follow it. The owlbear is controlled more than the party may yet realise.

**On Old Brindle:** If the party rolled encounter #17 on the wandering table and Brindle appeared, he may have passed through this area. If the referee wishes, Old Brindle has left a small ward at the north edge of the timber yard, a stone circle, the rocks placed in a druidic pattern that a Ranger or Druid recognises as a slow ward against corrupted movement. It has been partially broken, the stones kicked aside. The disruption is recent. Brindle is ahead of the party, moving fast.
