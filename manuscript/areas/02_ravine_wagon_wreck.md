# Area 2: Ravine Wagon Wreck

> The ravine opens without warning — the forest floor simply ends and the ground drops fifteen feet to a rubble-strewn stream bed below. The smell hits before the sight does: blood, wet clay, the sharp alchemical bite of something that does not belong in a forest. Coach B lies split at the bottom, one wheel still suspended over the stream, turning slowly in the current. Boxes have burst open across the stone. White powder clings to every surface it touched. Below, something is moving.

---

## The Ravine

The ravine runs roughly east-west, fifteen feet deep at this crossing, its walls steep clay and exposed root. A shallow stream — six inches deep, cold — cuts the stone floor. The wreck of Coach B occupies most of the navigable width: the coach body has split along its spine, both axles broken, the driver's bench thrown thirty feet east. The mules are dead, still partially in harness, their coats dusted white where they fell.

**Descent.** The east wall has a natural ledge eight feet down and enough root-handholds to climb without a roll. The west wall is sheer clay. Dropping directly from the rim is a 15-foot fall (1d6 damage, save vs. paralyzation to halve). Characters in plate or heavy encumbrance must make a Dexterity check or take full damage regardless.

**Light.** The ravine bottom is shadowed at any hour. The powder on the wagon debris and the stream-soaked mud creates a faint ambient shimmer — not enough to see by, but enough to notice.

---

## What's Here

**Whelm.** The warhammer is at the base of the east wall, buried to half its head in the stream-side mud where it rolled after impact. A character searching the ravine floor finds it automatically in one turn. A dwarf character within thirty feet detects it without searching — not through any magical sense, but as an almost physical discomfort, like a tuning fork struck near bone. The hammer is cold to the touch regardless of how long it is held. It is not currently warm to any wielder. It will not refuse to be carried, but it will not cooperate with non-dwarves: attack rolls made with Whelm by a non-dwarf suffer a −2 penalty until the hammer has been formally introduced to its new wielder over the course of a day's use (referee's discretion). *This is where Area 1's cold impact stone was pointing.*

**The three dead.** Three of Sorel Vane's crew lie in the wreck. The owlbear killed them during the attack that destroyed Coach B — claw marks across torso and head, consistent with the beast's track evidence at Area 1. They still have their equipment: leather armour, shortswords, belt pouches with 3d6 silver and 1d6 gold between them. One carries a folded paper: a partial ledger in coded shorthand (Intelligence check to partially decode) that references a rendezvous at *the camp* and a contact identified only as *K*. The ledger lists a quantity — *eight (8) tubes, confirmed* — and a date three days ago.

**The dust cache.** One crate survived the impact largely intact, its iron strapping holding. Its false bottom — a second layer of bladder-skin lining over a wax-sealed compartment — contains eight ceramic tubes of white plume dust, stoppered with beeswax. A character who disassembles the crate rather than simply opening it finds the hidden compartment automatically. The exterior of the crate bears Blink Tinderflip's shipping mark (the stylised T in a circle). Two of the eight tubes have been broken open; their contents are gone.

**The wagon manifest.** Pinned under the splintered driver's bench: a water-damaged but legible consignment document in Blink Tinderflip's handwriting. It lists three consignments across two coaches. Coach A: *Wave — sealed cask, handle with care.* Coach B: *W. — wrapped head, iron band.* *BR — wrapped, do not unwrap.* No further names. The manifest is addressed to *E. Forger, Ringstown*, which is one of Elnara's cover names.

---

## The Encounter: Mave Roon and Dort

Two of Sorel Vane's crew came to the wreck ahead of the party, looking for the scattered cargo. They arrived perhaps an hour ago. They found the dust cache. They sampled it.

**Mave Roon** took a single dose immediately on finding the cache. She is currently in the grip of it — sharp-eyed, fast, vibrating with something that feels like confidence. She has positioned herself behind the overturned coach body with the remaining six intact tubes in her pack. She will treat any approach as a threat to the cache. She is not irrational — she can be talked to — but her threat assessment is distorted and she will draw steel without hesitation if anyone reaches for her pack. She knows the cache belongs to Sorel Vane and that handing it over represents a significant professional failure. She also, quietly, does not want to stop feeling the way she currently feels.

**Dort** took a second dose forty minutes ago when the first began to fade. He is in backlash, rolling badly across multiple symptoms simultaneously. He is scratching the letter K into the ravine wall with his belt knife in an obsessive, methodical pattern — not one K, but dozens, filling a four-foot section of exposed clay. He periodically turns to announce to no one: *"I am Keraptis. I found it first."* He may attack the party, or Mave, or simply ignore both; his attention flickers between the wall and the nearest warm body. When he attacks, he is effective — the dust-enhanced reflexes survive even as the mind fragments.

**If Mave is wounded** she may grab a third tube from her pack and attempt to consume it, which the referee should telegraph clearly and allow the party one action to interrupt. If she takes a third dose, her bonuses persist but the referee should roll immediately on the backlash table at end of next round regardless of the duration — triple-dosing accelerates the crash.

**If Dort is restrained or knocked unconscious,** Mave loses her nerve and may attempt to bargain. She knows Sorel Vane's name, the location of the logging camp (Area 3), and that the rendezvous was supposed to happen the previous night. She does not know why Sorel has not appeared.

---

### Mave Roon *(dust-enhanced)*
**Fighter 2**, Human, Female
**AC** 5 *(leather + Dex)*, **HD** 2, **hp** 13
**Attacks** 1/round: shortsword +4 to hit, 1d6+3 damage *(dust bonuses included)*
**Saving Throws** Death/Poison 12, Wands 13, Paralyzation 14, Breath 15, Spells 16 *(+2 from dust)*
**Morale** 10 *(+2 from dust)*
**Equipment** Leather armour, shortsword, pack with 6 doses white plume dust, 14 gp, partial map of south approach trails

*Without dust:* +2 to hit, +1 damage, morale 8. Saving throws at listed values minus 2.

**Tactics:** Uses the wrecked coach body as cover (AC 3 against missile fire from above). Will not break and run while she has the cache; will attempt to flee east along the ravine floor if reduced below 4 hp.

---

### Dort *(deep backlash)*
**Fighter 2**, Human, Male
**AC** 6 *(leather)*, **HD** 2, **hp** 11
**Attacks** 1/round: handaxe +4 to hit, 1d6+3 damage *(dust bonuses)*
**Saving Throws** as Mave *(+2 from dust, actively ongoing second dose)*
**Morale** — *(ignore morale; Dort does not check morale and does not flee)*
**Equipment** Leather armour, handaxe, belt knife, 7 sp

*Backlash symptoms active:* Dort suffers a −1 penalty to AC against attacks he does not see coming (referee's discretion) due to fractured attention. He will spend his first action each round not attacking if there is an exposed surface within reach to carve. On a roll of 1 on his attack die, he has struck the ravine wall instead of his target.

**Tactics:** Dort does not have tactics. He moves toward the nearest perceived threat and attacks. He does not use cover. He will not stop when reduced to 1 hp — he must be knocked unconscious or restrained.

## Referee Notes

**On what happened here:** Coach B was struck by the owlbear mid-transit. The impact carried the coach over the ravine edge. Of the six smugglers Sorel had positioned along the road as outriders and spotters, three died in the chaos — two in the fall, one from the owlbear's follow-through. Mave and Dort survived by being thrown clear into the undergrowth. A sixth smuggler fled south immediately and is unaccounted for — Sorel knows someone ran but not who. The three dead on the ravine floor are the ones who didn't make it out in any direction. Mave and Dort reached the ravine floor and found the wreck intact enough to search before the rest of Sorel's crew could arrive from the south. They have been here roughly an hour.

**On Whelm's personality:** Whelm does not speak, rattle, or glow. Its personality is expressed entirely through temperature and weight. In the hands of a dwarf, it is comfortable — almost familiar, the way a well-balanced tool feels. In any other hands, it is subtly wrong: the balance seems slightly off, the grip slightly cold, as though the hammer is merely tolerating the arrangement. This is not mechanical. Do not make it mechanical. Let it be unsettling without a rule attached.

**On the dust cache:** Eight doses is a significant haul. Two are already gone (consumed by Mave and Dort). The remaining six represent real value to Elnara as evidence and to Sorel Vane as a professional loss. If the party attempts to sell the cache in Ringstown, Kelpwick at Thingizzard's Sundries will offer 5 gp per dose and ask no questions. Elnara will confiscate the cache as evidence if shown it, without payment, unless the party negotiates first.

**On the coded ledger:** A successful Intelligence check reads it as: a number of doses, a destination (*the camp*), a contact (*K*), and a date that has now passed. The *K* here does not refer to Keraptis — it is Sorel Vane's own coded shorthand for a contact whose name the referee should leave undefined until Area 3. The party may assume it refers to Keraptis; this is wrong but useful misinformation.

**On the manifest:** If the party recognises the cover name *E. Forger* and connects it to Elnara, they have more leverage with her than she will be comfortable with. Elnara will not confirm or deny the name unless pressed hard. She will add 10 gp to the commission without explanation if the party returns the manifest to her.

**On pace:** The encounter with Mave and Dort should feel dangerous and slightly chaotic — Dort's unpredictability is the engine. A party that handles this efficiently has earned the information Mave carries. A party that handles it messily may find themselves short on resources before Area 3. This is the adventure's first real test of party decision-making, not just combat capacity.
