# Area 2: Ravine Wagon Wreck

> The ravine opens without warning, the forest floor simply ends and the ground drops fifteen feet to a rubble-strewn stream bed below. The smell hits before the sight does: blood, wet clay, the sharp alchemical bite of something that does not belong in a forest. Coach B lies split at the bottom, one wheel still suspended over the stream, turning slowly in the current. Boxes have burst open across the stone. White powder clings to every surface it touched. Below, something is moving.

---

## The Ravine

The ravine runs roughly east-west, fifteen feet deep at this crossing, its walls steep clay and exposed root. A shallow stream, six inches deep, cold, cuts the stone floor. Three wrecks occupy the ravine floor, and they are not the same age. The fresh one, Coach B, dominates: the coach body has split along its spine, both axles broken, the driver's bench thrown thirty feet east. The mules are dead, still partially in harness, their coats dusted white where they fell. Behind and beneath the Coach B debris, partially obscured by days of mud accumulation and the larger wreck's spread, is an older ruin: a smaller, unmarked transport vehicle, no livery, no commercial strapping, the timber grey and swollen with a week of stream-wet. The second wreck settled deeper into the ravine floor and the stream has begun to flow around it. It has been here longer than Coach B by several days at minimum. To the west, half-buried under the stream mud and barely distinguishable from the surrounding rock, bleached splinters of an unmarked vehicle frame protrude from the bank — older than either wreck by months, with no livery and no fittings remaining, the timber so long submerged it has gone pale and soft.

**Descent.** The east wall has a natural ledge eight feet down and enough root-handholds to climb without a roll. The west wall is sheer clay. Dropping directly from the rim is a 15-foot fall (1d6 damage, save vs. paralyzation to halve). Characters in plate or heavy encumbrance must make a Dexterity check or take full damage regardless.

**Light.** The ravine bottom is shadowed at any hour. The powder on the wagon debris and the stream-soaked mud creates a faint ambient shimmer, not enough to see by, but enough to notice.

---

## What's Here

**Whelm.** The warhammer is at the base of the east wall, buried to half its head in the stream-side mud where it came to rest after striking the road above (Area 1) and rolling east into the ravine in the days since. A character searching the ravine floor finds it automatically in one turn. A dwarf character within thirty feet detects it without searching, not through any magical sense, but as an almost physical discomfort, like a tuning fork struck near bone. The hammer is cold to the touch regardless of how long it is held. It is not currently warm to any wielder. It will not refuse to be carried, but it will not cooperate with non-dwarves: attack rolls made with Whelm by a non-dwarf suffer a −2 penalty until the hammer has been formally introduced to its new wielder over the course of a day's use (referee's discretion). *This is where Area 1's cold impact stone was pointing.*

**The three dead.** Three of Sorel Vane's crew lie in the wreck. The owlbear killed them during the attack that destroyed Coach B, claw marks across torso and head, consistent with the beast's track evidence at Area 1. They still have their equipment: leather armour, shortswords, belt pouches with 3d6 silver and 1d6 gold between them. One carries a folded paper: a partial ledger in coded shorthand (Intelligence check to partially decode) that references a rendezvous at *the camp* and a contact identified only as *K*. The ledger lists a quantity, *eight (8) tubes, confirmed*, and a date that was last night.

**The dust cache.** One crate survived the impact largely intact, its iron strapping holding. Its false bottom, a second layer of bladder-skin lining over a wax-sealed compartment, contained eight ceramic tubes of white plume dust, stoppered with beeswax. A character who disassembles the crate rather than simply opening it finds the hidden compartment automatically. The exterior of the crate bears Blink Tinderflip's shipping mark (the stylised T in a circle). Two tubes remain in the compartment, both cracked open and emptied, Mave found the cache before the party arrived and cracked one tube for herself, then watched Dort crack a second. The remaining six intact tubes are in Mave's pack, not the crate.

**The Revocation.** Wedged beneath the collapsed packing frame of the older vehicle, half-buried in stream mud: a lead-lined wooden case, its wax seal intact, roughly the size of a large book. The lead lining is why the false Keraptis has not found it through scrying — he knows it exists and cannot locate it. The case is heavier than it looks. A character who searches the older vehicle's debris specifically — rather than the Coach B wreck, which dominates attention — finds it automatically in one turn. Mave and Dort did not find it; it is under the older vehicle's debris, not the Coach B wreck, and they had no reason to search something that predates their operation by a week.

A character who breaks the seal finds a flat stone tablet, eighteen inches by twelve, inscribed on both faces in a cramped and precise archaic hand. The language is Old Suloise; a Magic-User or a character with knowledge of ancient languages can read it with a successful Intelligence check, though full comprehension requires an hour's study. The gist, available on a partial success: a formal declaration in the first person, voiding the legitimacy of all mind-copies made after a specific date. The full text, on a full success or after study: the declaration is Keraptis's own, written before his death. It names the failsafe conditions under which each copy of his mind would collapse — the original built a killswitch into his copies and committed it to stone. The false Keraptis has been looking for this document for the duration of his operation in the Thicket. The dust, the owlbear, the smuggling network: at some level, all of it was a search.

**Blink's manifest.** Pinned under the splintered driver's bench of Coach B: a water-damaged but legible consignment document in Blink Tinderflip's handwriting. It covers two coaches and two consignments. Coach A: *Wave, sealed cask, handle with care.* Coach B: *Correspondence case, oilskin-wrapped, deliver intact.* No further names. The manifest is addressed to *E. Forger, Ringstown*, which is one of Elnara's cover names.

**The weapon-convoy manifest.** Inside the older vehicle, wedged beneath the collapsed packing frame: a second document, older and more water-damaged, written in a different hand — precise, educated, the kind of handwriting that belongs to someone who files reports. It lists three items. *W. — secured, iron band. Wrapped head, do not remove wrapping.* *BR — secured. Do not unwrap under any circumstances.* *R. — cased, lead seal. Do not open.* No shipping name. No commercial mark. No address. A routing note in a different ink at the bottom reads: *Proceed north. Staging confirmed. — E.* The handwriting on that last line is not the same as the manifest body.

## The Encounter: Mave Roon and Dort

Two of Sorel Vane's crew came to the wreck ahead of the party, looking for the scattered cargo. They found the dust cache. They sampled it.

**Mave Roon** took a single dose moments ago — she is still coming up on it, sharp-eyed and fast. She has positioned herself behind the overturned coach body with the remaining six intact tubes in her pack. She will treat any approach as a threat to the cache. She is not irrational, she can be talked to, but her threat assessment is distorted and she will draw steel without hesitation if anyone reaches for her pack. She knows the cache belongs to Sorel Vane and that handing it over represents a significant professional failure. She also, quietly, does not want to stop feeling the way she currently feels.

**Dort** took a second dose perhaps a minute ago when the first wore off. He is in backlash, rolling badly across multiple symptoms simultaneously. He is scratching the letter K into the ravine wall with his belt knife in an obsessive, methodical pattern, not one K, but dozens, filling a four-foot section of exposed clay. He periodically turns to announce to no one: *"I am Keraptis. I found it first."* He may attack the party, or Mave, or simply ignore both; his attention flickers between the wall and the nearest warm body. When he attacks, he is effective, the dust-enhanced reflexes survive even as the mind fragments.

**If Mave is wounded** she may grab a third tube from her pack and attempt to consume it, which the referee should telegraph clearly and allow the party one action to interrupt. If she takes a third dose, her bonuses persist but the referee should roll immediately on the backlash table at end of next round regardless of the duration, triple-dosing accelerates the crash.

**If Dort is restrained or knocked unconscious,** Mave loses her nerve and may attempt to bargain. She knows Sorel Vane's name, the location of the logging camp (Area 3), and that the rendezvous was supposed to happen the previous night. She does not know why Sorel has not appeared.

### Mave Roon *(dust-enhanced)*
**Fighter 2**, Human, Female
**AC** 5 *(leather + Dex)*, **HD** 2, **hp** 13
**Str** 15, **Dex** 14, **Con** 12
**Attacks** 1/round: shortsword +4 to hit, 1d6+3 damage *(dust bonuses included)*
**Saving Throws** Death/Poison 12, Wands 13, Paralyzation 14, Breath 15, Spells 15 *(+2 from dust)*
**Morale** 10 *(+2 from dust)*
**Equipment** Leather armour, shortsword, pack with 6 doses white plume dust, 14 gp, partial map of south approach trails

*Without dust:* +2 to hit, +1 damage, morale 8. Saving throw targets without dust: Death/Poison 14, Wands 15, Paralyzation 16, Breath 17, Spells 17.

**Tactics:** Uses the wrecked coach body as cover (AC 3 against missile fire from above). Will not break and run while she has the cache; will attempt to flee east along the ravine floor if reduced below 4 hp.

### Dort *(deep backlash)*
**Fighter 2**, Human, Male
**AC** 6 *(leather)*, **HD** 2, **hp** 11
**Str** 13, **Dex** 11, **Con** 12, **Int** 9, **Wis** 8, **Cha** 9
**Attacks** 1/round: handaxe +2 to hit, 1d6+1 damage
**Saving Throws** Death/Poison 14, Wands 15, Paralyzation 16, Breath 17, Spells 17
**Morale** *(ignore morale; Dort does not check morale and does not flee)*
**Equipment** Leather armour, handaxe, belt knife, 7 sp

*Backlash symptoms active:* Dort suffers a −1 penalty to AC against attacks he does not see coming (referee's discretion) due to fractured attention. He will spend his first action each round not attacking if there is an exposed surface within reach to carve. On a roll of 1 on his attack die, he has struck the ravine wall instead of his target.

**Tactics:** Dort does not have tactics. He moves toward the nearest perceived threat and attacks. He does not use cover. He will not stop when reduced to 1 hp, he must be knocked unconscious or restrained.

*Referee note:* Dort's double dose produced multiple failed saves — treat him as having rolled results 1 and 4 on the backlash table simultaneously. The module explicitly acknowledges this is exceptional; double-dosing stacks backlash rolls.

## Referee Notes

**On what happened here:** Two separate events converge in this ravine. First: Elnara's weapon convoy was attacked at the forest edge road (Area 1) by the owlbear, which had found an earlier dust cache and was already altered. The convoy's vehicle rolled or was dragged into the ravine; the operatives were killed and their bodies removed by the silk-wearing operative (Varek Sorn — see NPC section) before anyone else arrived. Whelm fell from the vehicle into the stream-side mud. Blackrazor was carried north by the owlbear. Second, days later: Coach B was struck by the owlbear on the road during the session opening, and its debris ended up in the ravine. Of the six smugglers Sorel had positioned as outriders, three died in the Coach B chaos, two in the fall, one from the owlbear's follow-through. Mave and Dort survived by being thrown clear. A sixth smuggler fled south and is unaccounted for, Sorel knows someone ran but not who. Mave and Dort reached the ravine floor and found the Coach B wreck intact enough to search. They did not examine the older vehicle thoroughly; the larger, fresher wreck drew their attention. They have been here roughly an hour.

**On the weapon convoy:** Elnara authorized the movement of Whelm, Blackrazor, and the Revocation north through the Thicket in a single convoy, acting under Shield Lands intelligence backing she does not advertise. The weapons were bait — place what the false Keraptis most wants within reach of his network and close the trap when he emerges. The Revocation was the real prize being moved to a safer stronghold, away from the mountain and away from whatever intelligence network the false Keraptis had been running in Ringstown. The convoy was unmarked and routed through the Thicket to avoid the main road. It failed before reaching its staging point. The owlbear, already altered by earlier dust exposure, struck the convoy at the forest edge, killed the operatives, and the silk-wearing operative removed the bodies before anyone else arrived. Elnara has told no one in Ringstown. If the party shows her the weapon-convoy manifest, she will recognise the routing note in her own hand and will not confirm it is hers. She will add 10 gp to the commission without explanation and say nothing further. That response is, itself, a confirmation.

**On Whelm's personality:** Whelm does not speak, rattle, or glow. Its personality is expressed entirely through temperature and weight. In the hands of a dwarf, it is comfortable, almost familiar, the way a well-balanced tool feels. In any other hands, it is subtly wrong: the balance seems slightly off, the grip slightly cold, as though the hammer is merely tolerating the arrangement. This is not mechanical. Do not make it mechanical. Let it be unsettling without a rule attached.

**On the dust cache:** Eight doses is a significant haul. Two are already gone (consumed by Mave and Dort). The remaining six represent real value to Elnara as evidence and to Sorel Vane as a professional loss. If the party attempts to sell the cache in Ringstown, Kelpwick at Thingizzard's Sundries will offer 5 gp per dose and ask no questions. Elnara will confiscate the cache as evidence if shown it, without payment, unless the party negotiates first.

**On the coded ledger:** A successful Intelligence check reads it as: a number of doses, a destination (*the camp*), a contact (*K*), and a date that has now passed. The *K* here does not refer to Keraptis. It is a shorthand used in the network for the contact at the camp — whose identity the referee should leave undefined until Area 3. The party may assume it refers to Keraptis; this is wrong but useful misinformation.

**On the manifests:** Two documents, two operations. Blink's manifest covers only his cargo: Wave and the correspondence case, addressed to E. Forger. If the party recognises E. Forger and connects it to Elnara, they have leverage she will be uncomfortable with. She will not confirm or deny the name; adds 10 gp to the commission without explanation and says nothing further. The weapon-convoy manifest is a separate document from a separate operation, and its routing note signed *E.* is the more dangerous revelation: it connects Elnara not just to the cargo chain but to the plan that created the owlbear problem. Elnara will react to these two documents very differently if the party distinguishes between them.

**On pace:** The encounter with Mave and Dort should feel dangerous and slightly chaotic, Dort's unpredictability is the engine. A party that handles this efficiently has earned the information Mave carries. A party that handles it messily may find themselves short on resources before Area 3. This is the adventure's first real test of party decision-making, not just combat capacity.

**On the Coach A variant:** The session intro includes a branching outcome where Coach A (carrying Wave) may be struck instead of Coach B. Area 2 is written for the Coach B outcome. If Coach A was struck: the dead outriders, dust cache, and manifests in this ravine remain unchanged — they belong to the weapon convoy and to Coach B's separate impact, not to which of Blink's coaches was hit on the road. Wave becomes the first recovery objective on the road north, findable at Area 1 or in the forest verge. The only element to adjust is the "Coach B lies split at the bottom" read-aloud — if Coach A was struck, Coach B was never destroyed, and the party may know this. The weapon convoy wreck, the Revocation, and Whelm are here regardless.
