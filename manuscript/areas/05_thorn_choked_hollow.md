# Area 5: Thorn-Choked Hollow

> The trail north from the logging camp ends here, but you know that before you see the hollow, you know it from the smell, which is no longer something in the air but something the air is made of, cold and bitter and sweetly wrong. Then the ground drops away into a depression ringed by a wall of blackthorn eight feet high and three feet thick, grown so dense it might as well be stone. From inside comes a wet, rhythmic sound, not a breath exactly, but something with the cadence of breathing, the weight of it, the texture. And through a gap in the thorn wall you can see the floor of the hollow. The bones there are very clean. The white powder on the bones catches what little light comes through the canopy and glitters like scattered stars.

---

## Approaching the Hollow

The trail north from Area 3 requires no tracking roll. Two pine trunks are knocked flat and point the way like a compass needle. A character who has been following the adventure's threads arrives here already knowing what is at the end of it.

**The last fifty feet.** As the party closes on the hollow's perimeter, several things are observable without entering or making noise:

- **The smell intensifies.** At thirty feet the bitter-sweet odour crosses from ambient to oppressive. Characters with sensitive smell (elves, certain racial abilities, or anyone explicitly paying attention) detect a secondary note beneath it: something animal and sick, like a large creature running a sustained fever.

- **The silence.** The forest's absolute quiet, established in Area 4, does not end at the glade's edge. It follows the party here. There are no birds anywhere in this part of the Thicket.

- **The K-marks.** The exterior of the thorn wall, the face pointing toward the approaching party, has no K-marks. A character who circles the perimeter before entering will find that all the marks are carved on the *interior* face of the surrounding trees: visible from inside the hollow, invisible from outside. They were placed to be read by something already inside. The knife marks are precise, unhurried, deliberate. They are not fresh, they predate the owlbear's contamination by the scarring of the bark. Someone prepared this hollow before the beast arrived. It was not the owlbear.

- **The torn silk.** Snagged on the exterior thorn wall at the northwest gap (the owlbear's primary exit): a strip of fabric, dark green, fine quality. Silk, or very close to it. Neither a logger nor a field smuggler wears silk this far north. It is not Sorel Vane's people. It was left by whoever came to carve the interior marks. The fabric is not fresh, it has been here through at least one rain, but it is not rotted. It has a faint cold quality to the touch, like the impact stone at Area 1. The thread that leads from this cloth leads north, to the mountain.

- **Whelm's response.** A character carrying Whelm notices the hammer becoming noticeably heavier and colder as they close on the hollow, not dramatically, not enough to impede movement, but unmistakeable. By the time they reach the thorn wall, the hammer is as cold as the basin stones at Area 4 and its balance feels subtly wrong, as though it is orienting toward something. A dwarf character may feel this as a low vibration rather than temperature: the note struck in the impact stone at Area 1 is still ringing here, and this is where it came from.

## The Hollow

The depression is roughly forty feet across and eight feet deep, the walls made of packed earth, exposed root, and the interior face of the thorn ring. The floor is the flattened earth of long occupation: scraped bare in the centre, compressed by weight and repeated movement, with deep parallel gouges radiating from the northwest corner (the owlbear's resting place) where claws have dragged across the ground in what might be pacing, or distress, or both.

**The thorn wall.** Three feet thick, eight feet high. The thorns are blackthorn and hawthorn, grown into a single interlocked mass. The owlbear passes through this freely, it has a body dense enough to push through without significant damage. For the party:

- Medium creatures can squeeze through any of the three visible gaps (northwest, southeast, north) in one round, taking 1d3 piercing damage from thorns.
- A character with 18 or more Strength can push a gap wide enough for one person to pass freely (one round of effort, no damage to the pusher, gap remains for the rest of the encounter).
- Ranged attackers can fire over the top of the wall from higher ground, the hollow is depressed, so a character standing at the rim has partial height advantage.
- The wall is not flammable under normal conditions (the wood is green and sap-thick) but will burn with alchemist's fire or similar accelerants. Burning the wall fills the hollow with dense smoke.

**The northwest corner.** The owlbear's rest. A depression in the earth deeper than the rest of the hollow floor, shaped by the creature's repeated occupation: a bowl perhaps eight feet across, six inches below the surrounding ground, compressed to near-clay hardness. The surrounding earth is covered in shed white powder so thick it has formed a visible crust. A character who kneels near this depression and examines it may notice what Old Brindle would recognise immediately: the creature has been returning to the same spot compulsively, circling it before lying down, leaving the evidence of hundreds of identical arrivals. This is not territorial behaviour. It is obsessive. Whatever is in the owlbear's head has been pulling it back here.

**The bone pile.** East wall of the hollow, sorted by no visible logic: deer ribs stacked separately from leg bones, skulls turned outward to face the hollow interior, smaller remains (fox, hare) filling gaps like rubble. The arrangement is not random and is not natural predator behaviour. Old Brindle, if asked, says flatly that owlbears do not arrange bones. Something that uses the owlbear occasionally has arranged these.

**The crate.** Half-buried against the south wall: one of Blink Tinderflip's shipping crates, the painted oak dark red, the T-in-circle mark visible on the base. The lid is staved in from the inside, the contents were not extracted but erupted. The bladder-skin lining has been torn apart and licked clean. This is not the first dust the owlbear has eaten, but it is the most. This crate is from the early Sorel Vane shipment whose wagon broke open in the ravine months before this adventure begins ,  the original contamination event. The creature ate what spilled then and dragged the ruined crate back here in the way that crows carry shiny objects. That first exposure changed it and drew it back whenever it caught the scent of dust in the forest. The more recent Coach B crash brought it back to the ravine by smell, reinforcing the addiction through scattered traces at the wreck site; the intact tubes Mave secured from the hidden compartment were not what it consumed. The interior still smells strongly enough to trigger a save vs. Death/Poison for any character who puts their head inside (failure: euphoric light-headedness for 2d4 rounds, -1 penalty to Wisdom checks during this time).

**The Coach B debris.** The owlbear carried pieces of the carriage back here. A wheel, still with one axle stub attached. Three sections of painted dark-red panelling. A saddlebag with a broken buckle, containing 14 silver pieces and a road map from Critwall, one of the party's from the session zero run. If the party recognises the equipment, the moment should land.

## The White Plume Owlbear

**If the pool was cleansed (Area 4 completed):** The owlbear is present but restless, pacing the northwest corner in a tight circle. Its glow has diminished, not gone, but reduced from the session zero description to a faint shimmer at the edges of its feathers. The K-Whispers ability is suppressed and will not trigger this encounter. It has used one Dust Frenzy already today and has two remaining. It is still a wounded, contaminated eight-hit-die creature and will attack the party; it is simply not at its absolute worst.

**If the pool was not cleansed:** The owlbear is at full power, pacing aggressively, its beak clacking in a rhythmic pattern that the party may recognise from the wandering encounter entry as its "almost a name" vocalization. All three Dust Frenzies are available. K-Whispers activates with each Frenzy.

**Before it charges.** The first time the party has eyes on the owlbear, before it detects them or before it attacks, there is a single beat that does not belong to an animal: the creature pauses mid-pace and goes completely still. Not alert ,  *still*, the way a person goes still when they are listening for something they are afraid to hear. For one or two seconds it does not move, does not breathe visibly, and the glow at the edges of its feathers dims almost imperceptibly. Then it resumes pacing and the moment closes. A referee who wants this beat to land should pause before the combat description begins and ask the table: *"For just a moment, watching it, what do you notice?"* It does not look dangerous in that second. It looks like something waiting to be allowed to stop.

**Detecting the party.** The owlbear has exceptional senses, heightened further by the dust. It detects the party by smell at sixty feet, by sound at forty, and by sight at thirty (in daylight). If the party has approached carefully (no armour noise, no torches, from downwind, a ranger can advise), they may achieve surprise: roll 1 die, surprise on 1 or 2. Otherwise, the owlbear knows they are coming before they reach the thorn wall.

**Initiative.** The owlbear acts on its own initiative. It does not wait inside the hollow. On its first initiative it comes through the thorn wall toward the party with no hesitation. It has done this before.

**Hug:** If both claw attacks hit the same target in the same round, the owlbear seizes that creature and automatically inflicts 2d6 additional damage at the start of the following round. The victim may attempt to escape (Strength check at -4, or an ally may attack the owlbear to force it to release, any hit that deals 6 or more damage causes immediate release).

**Dust Frenzy (3/day):** At the start of its turn, the owlbear enters a frenzy. For that round it gains +2 to hit, +2 to damage, and makes one additional claw attack (four attacks total: claw/claw/claw/beak). At the end of that round it takes 1d4 damage from the physiological stress. The frenzy is visible before it triggers: the owlbear's hide blazes white-bright for one heartbeat. Characters who have seen this before (from the session zero event) recognise the sign.

**K-Whispers (suppressed if pool cleansed):** When the owlbear enters Dust Frenzy, it vocalises, a sound that is not quite a roar, not quite a word, but resolves for one terrible fraction of a second into the shape of a repeated syllable, the ghost of a name it cannot pronounce with its throat. All creatures within 30 feet must save versus spells or suffer a -1 penalty to attack rolls and saving throws for 1 round. A character who has previously heard this sound (session zero, or wandering encounter #5) has likely already had bad dreams about it.

**Tactics:** The owlbear prioritises the largest target, shifting to any character who successfully damages it for more than 5 points in a single hit. It does not retreat. It does not call for help. It has no fear response, because what is riding in its head does not understand fear. It uses Dust Frenzy when it has taken 15 or more cumulative damage, not before. If reduced below 15 hp, it begins using Dust Frenzy every round it is available regardless of damage threshold.

**Whelm vs. the owlbear.** Whelm was forged to be used against creatures of giant-class size. The owlbear, being Large and empowered to the mass of a small giant, triggers this property: a character wielding Whelm against the owlbear gains +1 to hit and +1 to damage. A dwarf wielding Whelm against the owlbear gains +2 to hit and +2 to damage. When Whelm strikes the owlbear, a faint ringing tone sounds, the same note as the impact stone at Area 1. It lasts one round and has no mechanical effect, but the owlbear's frenzy falters very slightly in that moment: describe a half-second of confusion in its eyes before the expression closes again.

## Alternate Resolution: Breaking the Control

The owlbear can be killed. That is the expected outcome, and there is nothing wrong with it. But the threads have been pointing at another possibility since Old Brindle said *perhaps you can save it,* and the obsidian disc has been in the party's possession since Area 3 for a reason. A party that skipped Area 3 or did not recover the disc has no alternate resolution available; the owlbear must be defeated through combat. This is a valid outcome. The disc-based resolution is a reward for thoroughness, not a required path.

**The obsidian disc.** The disc is warm. It is part of the network, the K-marks, the scrying pools, the control structure the false Keraptis built to direct the owlbear. It is a key, and the lock is the thing riding in the owlbear's head.

If a character holds the disc up and presents it clearly to the owlbear within fifteen feet, not throwing it, not threatening with it, displaying it, the owlbear stops. One full round, no attacks. Presenting the disc requires one action and draws no free attack. If the owlbear has already acted in the current round and struck before the character could present it, the pause takes effect at the start of the owlbear's next round; one round of attacks may land before the disc resolves. A visible tremor runs through its body, starting at the skull and moving down the spine. The bitter-sweet smell intensifies sharply, the false Keraptis is pushing back through whatever connection remains. The owlbear's eyes, for that one round, are not the eyes of an animal in pain. They are the eyes of something watching from a very long way away.

Then the disc cracks, a single clean fracture through the centre, and goes cold. The connection is severed. The owlbear resumes attacking on the following round, but it is fighting on its own now, with no voice in its head amplifying its rage. It fights slightly less effectively: -1 to attack rolls for the remainder of the encounter as it reorients to its own instincts.

**Administering the antitoxin.** During the one-round pause when the disc is presented, a character who moves to adjacent position and attempts to apply a dose of white plume dust antitoxin (from Kael Vorst's body at Area 3, or from Kelpwick at Thingizzard's Sundries) to the owlbear may do so. The owlbear may make one attack at -2 to hit against the character (confused, not helpless), and the character must make a Dexterity check to apply it correctly, the antitoxin must reach exposed tissue, which means either a wound site or the mucous membrane of the eyes or beak.

On success: the antitoxin begins working. The owlbear calms far faster than the antitoxin alone would produce ,  in 1d4+2 rounds, frenzy gives way to agitation, agitation gives way to confusion, confusion gives way to exhaustion. The speed is a consequence of the severed control connection: without the false Keraptis's psychic amplification sustaining the contamination, the antitoxin only needs to address the residual chemistry rather than fight the ongoing effect. At the end of this period the creature sits. It does not sleep immediately but does not attack. Its white glow fades over the following hour to nothing. It is, for the first time since the original contamination event, simply a very large owlbear sitting in a hollow, terribly tired.

On failure: the owlbear shakes the application off and refocuses its aggression on the character who attempted it. The disc is still cracked and the connection still severed; the -1 attack penalty still applies. The antitoxin is not wasted, a second attempt can be made on the next round of the disc's effect, but the disc is spent. There is no second hesitation. The opportunity has passed.

**Aftermath of a calmed owlbear.** The creature will not interact meaningfully with the party. It is alive and no longer a threat in this location. It can be left in the hollow. If Old Brindle is told what happened, he will come to the hollow and begin longer druidic work. He will not promise recovery. He says only: *"It has been itself for a long time. What was put in it is gone. Whether what was there before has survived, that takes longer than a day to know."*

## What's Here

**Treasure type C (incidental).** Scattered among the bones and debris: 120 gp in mixed coinage from various victims, a jasper pendant worth 15 gp, a silver hand mirror with an etched back (25 gp), and a shortsword of above-average quality (treat as +1 for balance purposes, though it is not magical, a master smith's work). These were not hoarded; they are simply present, the residue of the hollow's long history as a place where things ended.

**The crate's seal fragment.** Readable with a successful Intelligence check: beneath the Tinderflip mark is a secondary impression, a different stamp applied first and partially obscured. It shows a stylised mountain peak with a single vertical line, not Blink's mark. This secondary stamp was applied before the crates left their point of origin. Its origin is underground. The party may or may not recognise it now; they will recognise it later, if this adventure is a prologue to what follows.

**The interior K-marks.** Twenty-seven distinct marks on the interior thorn trees. A character who spends one turn counting and examining them finds they are not random: they are arranged in a specific spatial pattern, radiating from the owlbear's rest-depression as a centre point, with four larger marks at cardinal directions and smaller marks between. It is a compass. It is also, a Magic-User or Cleric may recognise, a containment diagram, not one that traps the owlbear inside, but one that provided it with a fixed territory to return to. Whoever placed these marks knew where the owlbear would den before it chose this hollow. The hollow was prepared for the owlbear, not by the owlbear.

## Referee Notes

**On the moral weight.** The owlbear did not choose this. Everything in the adventure has been pointing at this: the gnome's warning, Old Brindle's exhaustion, Pirrel carving K-marks she cannot control, Dort announcing to no one that he is Keraptis. The dust doesn't ask permission. The party arrives here knowing that and should feel it. Run the owlbear as dangerous and terrible, but let the moment of the disc's crack, and the owlbear's one round of confused self, land.

**On killing the owlbear.** If the party kills the owlbear without attempting the alternate resolution, do not punish them for it. They are within their rights. Elnara will pay the commission without comment. Old Brindle will be quiet for a long time when told, and will then say: *"I hoped."* That is all. The adventure is complete. What the false Keraptis sent this owlbear to guard, the hollow, the control structure, the prepared territory, is also complete, and its purpose is uncertain. The question of what was supposed to happen here next is a thread forward.

**On Whelm's future.** Whelm is not at home in the party's possession. It is cooperative because the alternative is lying in a ravine, but it is waiting. A dwarf character who has wielded it through this adventure has received something it will not give to just anyone: the sound of it striking the owlbear and ringing, the note it wants to make against worthy opposition. This is the beginning of a relationship. Whelm wants to go north, not because the false Keraptis calls it, but because that is the direction of its maker, its original place, and the unfinished business that belongs to a weapon that has been away from home too long.

**On what was supposed to happen here next.** The hollow was prepared. The owlbear was contaminated and directed. The K-marks form a containment diagram with the owlbear at its centre. Kael Vorst was eliminated before he could collect the second cache. Someone with silk clothing and a cold obsidian disc came here, placed the marks, and left ,  this is Varek Sorn (see NPC section), who preceded the party through the Thicket by days. If the party follows these threads to their source, north, underground, they will find White Plume Mountain, and the false Keraptis who has been watching them through the pool, through the marks, through the owlbear's borrowed eyes since they first crossed the treeline.

**On the conclusion.** After this area, the party has addressed the adventure's stated objectives: the beast is either dead or calmed, the dust cache has been found and can be destroyed or secured, Elnara's commission is earned. Area 6 (Game Trails) leads to Blackrazor, and Area 7 (the Riddle Cave) is a discoverable bonus. Neither is required to end the adventure. The party may leave. The plume above the mountain will still be visible from the south road at dusk, and the face in it will still be watching.
