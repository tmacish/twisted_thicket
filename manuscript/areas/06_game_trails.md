# Area 6: Game Trails

> Every path in the Thicket looks temporary, as though made yesterday by fear and forgotten by morning, a deer track here, a boot print there, the old logging cut that has half-closed over itself in one season of growth. But the mud remembers more carefully than that. Stand still for a moment and look at the ground: tracks overlay tracks overlay tracks, the record of everything that has moved through this forest in the past week compressed into three inches of clay and pine duff. Something very large has passed through here multiple times. Something very small has left knotted cords at chest height across the path. And from somewhere to the northeast, at the edge of hearing, there is a sound that is not quite sound, more like the memory of a note played in a room you are no longer in.

---

## Reading the Trails

The game trails are not a single location but a network of interconnected paths between the ravine (Area 2), the logging camp (Area 3), the hollow (Area 5), and the Riddle Cave (Area 7). A party that takes the time to read the ground before moving finds the following. Each layer can be identified by anyone who looks; a Ranger reads all of them without effort.

**The owlbear's circuit.** The same three-toed prints from Area 1 appear here repeatedly, not one passage, but many, overlapping at different angles, some dry and crumbling, some still wet. The owlbear has made this circuit dozens of times. Its routes are not random: it moves from the northeast (Area 5) to the south (Area 1), passes through, and returns north. Occasionally a route cuts east toward the ravine. The pattern is territorial, but the territory it describes is not a natural owlbear range, it is too regular, too repeated, as though the beast has been patrolling rather than hunting.

**The gnome cords.** At irregular intervals along the main north-south trail, thin cords of twisted plant fibre are strung between trees at approximately chest height. They are knotted in specific patterns. A character who reads Gnomish, or who succeeds on an Intelligence check having been told that mountain gnomes use knotted trail cords for communication, can interpret them: *large beast, north, do not follow the light, three days*. The cords are not all the same age. The oldest are a week old. The most recent was tied within the last day. The gnomes have been updating their warnings as the situation develops.

**The silk-wearer's tracks.** Overlapping the owlbear's most recent circuit: boot prints, narrow-soled, a person of average weight moving with deliberate unhurried steps. The prints enter from the north, cross the main trail twice, and exit north toward the basalt foothills the drovers call the Northern Rise, the broken upland at the foot of White Plume Mountain. They are the same profile as would be left by whoever wore the green silk snagged at the hollow's thorn wall. A Ranger or character with tracking proficiency can determine: the prints are 18 to 24 hours old. The person who made them was not running, not hiding, and did not deviate around obstacles, they walked directly through the Thicket with the confidence of someone who knew exactly where they were going.

**The scavengers' camp.** On the east side of the trail, partially concealed by a deadfall: a camp fire that has been properly smothered (buried, not just abandoned), a bedroll stuffed under the deadfall, and three crossbow bolt cases stamped with a Critwall smith's mark. Someone has been here for at least two nights and is either still nearby or recently moved.

## Blackrazor

The greatsword is embedded point-first in the trunk of a large white oak thirty feet east of the main trail, three feet off the ground. It did not fall here. The angle of entry, slightly downward, the blade driven deep, is consistent with a large animal striking the trunk at speed and the sword continuing when the animal stopped. The owlbear hit this tree hard enough to crack a root. The sword went through the bark and into the heartwood and stayed.

**What the tree shows.** Above the blade: dead. The bark has gone grey and dry, the branches above the impact point leafless, the wood desiccated as if by a summer drought compressed into a week. Below the blade: alive, healthy, if slightly confused in its growth, as though the tree is leaning away from the sword without quite moving. The dead zone is spreading upward, a half-inch a day, approximately. The tree will be dead to the crown inside a month. Whatever Blackrazor drinks from, it has been drinking from this oak since the owlbear left it here.

**Detecting it.** A character who was at Area 1 and experienced the Wisdom-detectable pull from the northeast felt this sword. That pull becomes impossible to ignore within sixty feet, not a compulsion, not a charm, but a presence, the way a person standing behind you in a quiet room is a presence. Characters who have previously handled Blackrazor (impossible in this adventure unless the referee has arranged it from a prior session) can walk to it blindfolded.

A character with Whelm, approaching the oak: Whelm becomes cold enough to be uncomfortable in the hand. Not painful, not dropping temperature, simply wrong. The two weapons are aware of each other. They have history. Whelm does not want to be near Blackrazor, and its expression of this is to become a tool you want to put down.

**Retrieving it.** The blade is driven four inches into heartwood. Pulling it free requires either a Strength check at -2 or two characters making normal Strength checks in a coordinated effort. It comes free cleanly, no resistance from the wood, no special ceremony required. The oak does not visibly react, but the dead bark above the wound crumbles slightly, releasing a fine grey dust that is not white plume dust. It is simply dead wood. Blackrazor killed this tree the way it kills things: thoroughly and without noticing.

**What Blackrazor feels like.** Picking it up is not the same as picking up a sword. The first thing is the weight, slightly less than expected for a blade this size, which is wrong. The second is the sound: not from the blade but from somewhere inside the person holding it, a note below the threshold of hearing that is felt in the sternum before it is heard anywhere else. It is not unpleasant. That is, as rumour #86 correctly notes, the problem.

The sword does not speak. It does not communicate preferences or issue warnings. It simply hungers, and holding it means being adjacent to that hunger, and after a few rounds of carrying it a character may notice that the hunger and their own appetites have begun to feel similar. This has no mechanical expression in a short adventure. In a campaign, it would.

**Blackrazor in combat.** If used against the owlbear at Area 5: Blackrazor functions normally, it is a +3 greatsword for purposes of this adventure. A character who kills the owlbear with Blackrazor must save versus spells. On a failed save, the contaminated life-essence draining through the blade fills them with one round of the full white plume dust effect: +2 to everything, the smell of bitter sweetness around them, and whatever the owlbear was experiencing when it died passing through them briefly. This passes. The memory of it may not.

## The Dead Smuggler

Forty feet west of the main trail, propped upright against a pine with his back to the bark and his legs straight out before him: a man in Sorel Vane's crew colours, dead, arranged to look as though he sat down to rest. His hands are folded in his lap. A sealed ceramic crate is wedged between his knees. He would look peaceful except for the letter carved into his forehead, precise, deliberate, post-mortem, the cut made by a narrow blade held very steadily.

**Who he is.** This is Orn, one of Sorel Vane's four crew members at the logging camp (Area 3). If the party met Sorel's people, they recognise him. If they didn't, his Critwall-made short sword and the camp-mud on his boots are connective evidence. He was sent from the logging camp on an errand Sorel will not remember authorising, because Sorel didn't. Someone else sent him. He went. He did not come back.

**How he died.** A healer's examination or a successful Intelligence check reveals: a single narrow wound to the back, below the left shoulder blade, angled upward. The same profile as Kael Vorst at Area 3. The same killer, the same blade, the same post-mortem arrangement. The K carved into Kael's forehead and the K carved into Orn's forehead were made by the same hand. Kael was the contact the party had evidence for. Orn is evidence that the killer is still working, and still moving north.

**Fresh tracks near the body.** A character who examines the ground immediately around the pine finds a separate set of narrow-soled boot prints distinct from the 18-to-24-hour transit tracks described in the trail reading above. These are recent ,  made within the last few hours, the mud still soft in the impressions. They approach from the north, stop at the pine, and return north. The operative came back through after Orn followed the note.

**The crate.** The seal on its lid matches neither Blink Tinderflip's mark nor the mountain-peak secondary stamp from Area 5, it bears a plain wax impression with no design, pressed by a thumb. Inside: 3 doses of white plume dust in ceramic tubes and a folded paper, written in a careful hand. The paper is not coded. It reads:

> *For the attention of the receiving party: the second cache has been relocated. The original staging point (the camp supply shed) was compromised. These three tubes are the remainder. The balance of the shipment moved north ahead of schedule. Find the cave and follow the markers. Do not use the obsidian method of entry, that key is spent.* (See Area 7 referee notes for which disc this refers to ,  it is the operative's disc, not Kael Vorst's.)

The note was written for Orn to deliver. It was taken from him after he died and placed back in the crate, where it would be found with the body. Whoever wrote it, the silk-wearing operative, wanted the party to find this, or at least did not care if they did. They are not afraid of being understood.

**If Sorel Vane is alive and the party returns this information to him:** Sorel is very quiet for a long moment. Then: *"He was my cousin. Fourteen years."* He will answer two additional questions about the smuggling operation without his usual caution, because at this point caution seems beside the point.

## The Rival Scavengers

The smothered camp fire belongs to a group from Critwall: four armed individuals and their leader, currently split between the camp site and a position watching the main trail from cover. They have been here two days. They came looking for scattered cargo from the Coach B wreck. They have found nothing useful, become increasingly nervous about the noises from the north, and are now debating whether to leave.

They will attempt to ambush the party if the party approaches from the south (they will assume the party is Sorel's crew). If the party approaches from any other direction, or if someone announces their presence before walking into the ambush arc, the scavengers will demand to know who the party is and what they want.

**Negotiating.** The scavengers want to leave. They do not want to fight anyone who has clearly survived deeper in the Thicket than they have gone themselves. If the party can demonstrate:
- They are not Sorel's crew, AND
- They have found something the scavengers came to find (any piece of Coach B cargo, any dust), AND
- They are not planning to report the scavengers' presence to Elnara,

...the scavengers will sell what they know for 10 gp total: the partial buyer network in Critwall (names that partially overlap with the coded ledger from Area 2), the fact that a second group passed through heading north yesterday (the silk-wearer, not the party, someone else), and the location of a knotted gnome cord they found but cannot read.

If the party has the lost gnome from the encounter below and can translate the cord: it reads differently from the others. The others said *do not follow the light.* This one, the most recently tied, says *the cave is still safe.* This is directions to Area 7, marked for gnomes who know the code.

## The Lost Gnome

Somewhere in the game trails, referee's choice of timing, or triggered when the party is clearly heading north without a clear destination, is Nib, a single gnome berry-gatherer separated from her group. She is not hiding exactly, but she has climbed a tree and is waiting for the world to calm down before descending.

She will not come down willingly. She will come down if offered food (she is hungry), if the party demonstrates they are not hostile (patience, calm voice, not drawing weapons), or if a gnome-speaking character addresses her in Gnomish directly.

She speaks Common but prefers not to. She will not discuss the mountain, the dust, or the owlbear's origin. She will discuss the forest, because the forest is her domain. She knows:
- The game trails to Area 7 precisely, she can lead the party there without any navigation rolls.
- The cord language, and will translate any knotted cord the party has found (including the scavengers' cord, if obtained: *"The cave is still safe."*).
- That something walked north through the main trail yesterday, moving fast, wearing something that did not smell like the forest. She did not see its face. She stayed very high in her tree.
- That there are three cave openings in the rocky outcrop north of here. Two are animal dens. One goes deeper than it should.

She will trade this knowledge, specifically, guiding the party to Area 7, for escort south to the forest edge when the party is finished. She does not want payment. She wants to go home.

*Referee note:* If the party encountered a lost gnome via wandering encounter table entry 13, that was Nib. If so, she recognises the party and will come down from her tree immediately ,  the survival hesitation is gone. She offers guidance to Area 7 as described, simply without the negotiation delay. Nib is a specific individual, not a generic encounter.

## What Else Is Here

**The dust trail (if Area 5 not yet visited).** North along the main trail: a smeared path of glittering white powder and three-toed prints, fresh enough that the edges have not yet dried. Following it north requires no tracking roll and leads directly to the thorn wall of Area 5 within one exploration turn.

**A gnome basket, abandoned.** Thirty feet off the east trail, a small wicker basket with a broken handle lies on its side. It contains six plumeberries, two of which have been crushed. The basket was dropped in haste. There is no blood nearby. The gnome who dropped it got away.

**The split oak where the owlbear turned.** One tree in the trail network bears evidence of the owlbear's session-zero return: claw marks from the road, running north along the bark at height. This is where the beast turned east after carrying Blackrazor, the gouge pattern from Area 1 ends here, and the owlbear's northbound track resumes from this point without the drag signature. This is where Blackrazor stopped moving with the owlbear and started being in the oak.

## Referee Notes

**On Blackrazor as a player decision.** Blackrazor is a legendary weapon with a will, and carrying it back to Elnara is not the same as carrying Wave or Whelm. Elnara will accept it, she wants all three weapons secured, but she will keep Blackrazor separately and will not let anyone handle it after the handoff. A character who has been carrying Blackrazor through the adventure may find this transaction more difficult than expected. The sword has not bonded to them. It has simply been adjacent to them long enough to feel familiar. This is not the same thing, but it can feel like it.

**On Whelm near Blackrazor.** If the party is carrying Whelm (Area 2) and retrieves Blackrazor here, both weapons are now in the same location. Whelm goes cold and stays cold. A dwarf carrying Whelm feels the hammer pulling slightly away from Blackrazor, not violently, not usefully, just a sustained lean in the opposite direction. If the party asks what this means, the honest answer is that they are two very old things with very different purposes and they do not like being near each other. This has no mechanical effect. It is simply true. A character may carry both weapons simultaneously; if they do, Whelm's cold becomes uncomfortable enough in the grip that most parties will naturally distribute the weapons to two carriers without being told to. This is the right instinct.

**On Orn's note and "the cave."** The cave referenced in the note is Area 7, the Riddle Cave of the Ash Tongue. The warning that "that key is spent" refers to the operative's own disc, which they used to bypass the face mechanism when they entered the cave two days earlier. Kael's disc, found on his body at Area 3, was a separate copy still intact when Kael died. By the time the party reads this note, both discs are spent, but for unrelated reasons: the operative's from opening the cave, Kael's from the party's use at Area 5 (if they used it there). A player who asks how the operative knew the disc would be spent has the right instinct but the wrong disc in mind. The note was written for a different reader and a different set of circumstances. The party is not the intended audience, and reading it as the audience they are, people who have already severed the control connection, gives them a significant intelligence advantage: whoever is north of here does not know the control apparatus has been disrupted.

**On the silk-wearing operative (Varek Sorn).** They passed through the game trails 18 to 24 hours ago on their main transit, setting up the note for Orn, killing Kael during the logging camp attack, and exiting north. When Orn followed the note north a few hours ago, the operative had not yet cleared the area entirely ,  they intercepted him in the game trails, killed him, arranged the body, and continued north. The 18-to-24-hour transit tracks and the fresh tracks near Orn's body are from two separate passages through the same ground. They passed north through this area and did not stop here. Their current position is Area 7, the Riddle Cave of the Ash Tongue, where they solved the puzzle, opened the cache, and chose to wait rather than continue. A party moving through the areas in sequence will find them there.

**On pacing.** Area 6 is not a destination, it is a transit zone that rewards the party for thoroughness. A party that moves through quickly finds Blackrazor and nothing else. A party that reads the trails, examines the dead smuggler, talks to the scavengers, and climbs the right tree finds the full picture of who is moving through this forest and in what direction. Both outcomes are valid. The second outcome is richer. Do not force the slow path, but reward it.
