# Session Introduction: The Road to Ringstown

*This section presents a structured opening scene for tournament play, convention one-shots, or as a cold open for any group. It establishes the party's connection to the three legendary weapons and provides an inciting incident before the adventure proper begins. Run this before handing out any maps.*

---

## The Contract

The player characters have been hired by **Blink Tinderflip**, a gnome they met operating out of **Critwall**, to drive and ride two carriages carrying three sealed crates of "sensitive cargo" along the road from Critwall to the frontier town of **Ringstown**. The players are the coachmen and outriders — there is no separate hired help. They are told only that the contents are fragile, valuable, and must be delivered intact to **Ranger-Captain Elnara** at the Ringstown gatehouse. They are not told what is inside. The pay is generous. Blink is pleasant, evasive, and has already left town.

The three crates contain, unknown to the party:

| Crate | Contents | Coach |
|-------|----------|-------|
| 1 | *Wave* (trident) | Coach A — lead carriage |
| 2 | *Blackrazor* (greatsword) | Coach B — rear carriage |
| 3 | *Whelm* (warhammer) | Coach B — rear carriage |

Divide the players as evenly as possible between the two coaches before the scene begins. Each coach needs at least one player on the driver's bench; the rest ride inside or on the running boards.

---

## Read-Aloud: The Approach

*Read this to all players before calling for dice.*

> Three days on the road from Critwall and the forest has closed in on both sides like a throat swallowing. The Twisted Thicket is what the drovers called it when they refused this contract, and now you understand why: the trees lean over the road, roots have buckled the old stones into a washboard, and the dark between the trunks is absolute. You have gone quiet. Those of you on the bench grip the reins a little tighter.
>
> Ahead — perhaps half a mile — you can see the warm scatter of lights that must be Ringstown. The gate torches are burning. You are almost there.
>
> Then something moves in the dark to the north. Not a sound exactly. A pressure. A disturbance in the tree line that the horses feel a full heartbeat before you do.

---

## Perception Check

Each player rolls **1d20 and adds their Wisdom modifier** (OSRIC/2E: Wisdom reaction/intuition adjustment, or a straight Wisdom check if modifiers are not in use). Sum the totals within each coach separately.

The coach with the **lower sum** failed to notice in time.

*On a tie, Coach B (the rear carriage) is struck.*

---

## The Strike

*Read the following to the players in the lower-scoring coach. Players in the surviving coach hear this from a distance.*

> The horses scream. There is a sound like a mill wheel shattering and then the carriage is simply gone — replaced by splinters, spinning crate-wood, and a shape that does not stop moving. It is enormous. It glitters. Where its hide catches the gate-light ahead it throws off cold white sparks, and it moves with a speed that makes no sense for something that large. A smell hits you — bitter, sharp, faintly sweet, wrong in the way that lightning smells wrong.
>
> It is through the tree line and gone before you can draw a weapon.

**Every player in the destroyed coach must Save vs. Paralyzation** (the 2E equivalent of a Dexterity save against a physical catastrophe).

| Result | Damage |
|--------|--------|
| Failed save | 2d6 trampling + 1d6 falling (3d6 total) |
| Successful save | 1d6 falling only |

The surviving coach and its crates are undamaged.

**Scattered crates:** Roll 1d6 for each crate from the destroyed coach.

| d6 | Result |
|----|--------|
| 1–4 | Intact but thrown from the road; can be found at Area 2 or Area 6 |
| 5–6 | Crate smashed open; contents visible on the road |

---

## Read-Aloud: Elnara at the Gate

> The gates of Ringstown are already open. A woman in ranger's leathers stands at the threshold with a lantern held high, and her expression as she looks past you at the wreckage in the road is not surprise. It is recognition.
>
> "That's the third carriage this tenday," she says. She looks at you, then at the crates — however many remain. Her jaw tightens. "You'd better come inside. And tell me everything Blink told you." She picks up one of the intact crates, turns it over in her hands. "Which I suspect was not much."

---

## Referee Notes

**On Blink Tinderflip:** Blink knows exactly what the weapons crates contain. He acquired Blackrazor, Wave, and Whelm through intermediaries connected to a prior expedition into White Plume Mountain and is routing them to Elnara for safekeeping before the Inheritor's agents can recover them. He withheld the details to prevent exactly the kind of loose talk that gets caravans ambushed. He is not currently reachable.

**On Blink's mark appearing on dust crates:** Blink Tinderflip runs a general freight operation out of Critwall — he moves cargo for anyone who pays and does not ask many questions about contents. The Inheritor's network has been using Blink's shipping infrastructure for the white plume dust trade for months, likely through Kael Vorst acting as a purchasing intermediary. Blink knew the dust shipments were contraband but treated them as someone else's problem. He may not have known the dust was connected to the same mountain operation as the weapons — or he may have. The dust crates and the weapons crates share the same marks because they share the same shipper. This is intentional misdirection on the Inheritor's part: mixing legitimate and criminal freight through the same visible network makes both harder to trace. If the party confronts Blink about this (unlikely in this adventure, as he is unreachable), he will be evasive. A referee running a sequel should decide in advance how much Blink actually knew.

**On the missing crates:** Any crates lost in the wreck are the party's first concrete objective inside the Thicket. The weapons will be found at Area 2 (the ravine wagon wreck) or Area 6 (game trails), depending on the scatter roll above. This ties the recovery mission directly to the exploration of the Thicket.

**On the owlbear:** The party gets no clean look at it. They know it was large, fast, and abnormally luminescent. This is their first encounter with the White Plume Owlbear and should feel like a force of nature — something that cannot be engaged, only survived. Save the full reveal for Area 5.

**On Elnara:** She will not share everything she knows at the gate. She confirms the weapons are real, confirms something is wrong in the Thicket, and will offer the party a contract to enter it — which is where the adventure proper begins.
