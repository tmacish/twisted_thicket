# Concluding the Adventure

The adventure ends once the White Plume Owlbear is slain or driven off and the remaining dust is either secured or destroyed.

- **If the dust is destroyed:** Local wardens and settlers are grateful, and rumors spread that the corruption in the forest was only a symptom of something worse in the mountain.
- **If the dust is preserved or sold:** The party profits, but the substance will continue to spread the schemes of the false inheritor beneath White Plume Mountain.
- **If the party uses the dust repeatedly:** End with a fever dream or whispered promise from an unseen intelligence in the north.

---

## Expanded Forest Tie-Ins

The Twisted Thicket should feel like the infected outer skin of White Plume Mountain rather than an isolated woodland. These additions deepen the mountain connection without changing the adventure's core structure.

### Gnome Berry-Gatherers

Generations of mountain gnomes still emerge from hidden vents and root-choked paths to gather plumeberries in the thicket. Signs of their passage include tiny wicker baskets, knotted trail cords, chalked K symbols, and small boot prints mixed with berry stains.

### Dead Gnoll's Eye Socket

This blasted hollow works well as a fallback camp, rumor site, or hidden cache point on the approach to White Plume Mountain.

### The Plume as Omen

At dusk, or after major disturbances of the dust, those looking north may briefly see a face forming in the vapor above White Plume Mountain.
