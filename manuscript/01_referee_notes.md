# Referee's Notes

## Tone and Style

This adventure works best when run as a mixture of wilderness horror, dark comedy, and old-school resource play. The beast is terrifying, but the absurdity of a hyper-aggressive owlbear empowered by magical narcotic dust should still come through. Lean into signs of excess — trees clawed to splinters, half-eaten smugglers, glowing sneezes of white powder, and confused witnesses recounting impossible speed and human-like shrieks.

---

## Hooks

1. **Warden's Request:** A ranger-captain named Elnara asks the party to investigate recent killings and destroy any strange alchemical substance found in the forest.
2. **Mercenary Contract:** A patron in town hires the party to recover several casks of contraband before the authorities seize them.
3. **Missing Persons:** A local notable offers a reward for the recovery of loggers and trappers missing in the Twisted Thicket.
4. **Mountain Rumors:** Several survivors claim that the distant plume above White Plume Mountain now forms a face at dusk, and that the beast sometimes screams words in a human tongue.
