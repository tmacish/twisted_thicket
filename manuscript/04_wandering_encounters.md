# Wandering Encounters

Check for a wandering encounter once per hour of travel or exploration, or whenever the party makes significant noise. Roll 1d6: on a 1 or 2, an encounter occurs. Roll 1d20 on the table below to determine what.

If the owlbear has been defeated or calmed, replace results 5, 11, and 20 with result 1.

**State-Dependent Results.** Several results become invalid as the adventure progresses. When the conditions below are met, replace the indicated result with result 1 (Unnatural Silence):

- **Result 3**, Remove once the party has made direct contact with Sorel's group at Area 3. After that, the party knows Sorel's location and a chance road encounter is no longer plausible. This result remains valid if the party bypassed Area 3 entirely. Roll 1d4 rather than 2d4 for crew count; Sorel's numbers are reduced after the road attack.
- **Result 4**, Remove once the party has resolved the scavenger camp in the game trails (Area 6).
- **Result 12**, This scene and the body at Area 6 represent the same person (Orn, from Sorel's crew). Use this result only if the party has entered the game trails but not yet found his body; if it fires earlier, hold it. Remove it once Orn has been found.
- **Result 17**, Remove once the party has located Old Brindle at Area 4. Before that point, his appearance here reflects him returning to the glade after a brief absence; he should reach Area 4 shortly after the party does. Once his two-day vigil at the pool is established, this result no longer applies.

| d20 | Encounter |
|-----|-----------|
| 1 | **Unnatural silence.** No encounter, but the forest is wrong, no birdsong, no wind. The party's animals will not calm for an hour. Something passed through recently. |
| 2 | **Giant weasels (1d4).** Driven from their burrows by trace dust exposure. Frothing, erratic, and faster than they should be. They pursue if the party retreats. |
| 3 | **Sorel Vane's crew (2d4 smugglers).** Searching the trail for the scattered crates from the wreck. Armed and nervous. Initially hostile; they can be negotiated with if the party demonstrates they want the dust destroyed rather than claimed. |
| 4 | **Rival scavengers (2d4).** A competing faction of opportunists from Critwall, armed with crossbows, who mistake the party for Sorel's crew. Hostile until corrected. If convinced the party is not Sorel's people, they will sell information about the smuggling network's structure for 10 gp. |
| 5 | **Distant shriek.** The owlbear cries out somewhere to the north, not a territorial call but something that sounds briefly, horribly, like a name. All characters are surprised on a roll of 1–2 in the next encounter this session. |
| 6 | **Dead cart-horse.** Still in harness, coat dusted white, eyes open. Very recently killed, not yet cold. There is no cart, no driver, and no trail leading away. |
| 7 | **White plume dust (1d3 doses).** A wax-sealed ceramic tube half-buried in the trail mud, stamped with a crude K. See the White Plume Dust section for effects. |
| 8 | **Giant rats (1d4).** Nesting in the ruins of a collapsed trapper's cache. The cache contains 2d6 days of rations, one dose of dust wrapped in oilcloth, and a coded note referencing a rendezvous at "the camp." |
| 9 | **Stirges (2d6).** A roosting colony in a hollow oak, disturbed by movement. They attack immediately and pursue for up to three rounds before breaking off. |
| 10 | **Gnome berry-gatherers (1d3).** They vanish into the undergrowth if approached directly. They leave behind a knotted warning cord hung at chest height across the path. A successful Wisdom check or ranger tracking roll reads it as: *large beast, north, do not follow the light.* |
| 11 | **The owlbear crosses the path.** Sixty feet ahead, moving at speed, trailing cold white sparks. It does not stop. It does not look at the party. It is gone in two rounds. No action is possible. The smell of bitter sweetness lingers for ten minutes. |
| 12 | **A dead smuggler,** propped upright against a tree as though sleeping. The letter K has been carved into his forehead, recent, deliberate, post-mortem. A sealed crate rests between his knees containing 3 doses of dust and a folded note. |
| 13 | **Lost gnome.** A single gnome berry-gatherer, separated from her group and clearly frightened. She will not speak of the mountain. She will trade directions to Area 7 (the Riddle Cave of the Ash Tongue) in exchange for escort to the forest edge. *This is Nib, who also appears as a fixed encounter in Area 6 (Game Trails). If the party encounters her here, she has the same knowledge and motivations described there. Remove this entry once Area 6 has been fully explored.* |
| 14 | **Corrupted vegetation.** The plants in a twenty-foot radius are bent toward an unseen centre, flowers open the wrong direction, bark split along lines that form partial letters. The ground underfoot is faintly warm. Any character who lingers more than one round must save vs. spells or feel an overwhelming urge to carve something into the nearest surface. |
| 15 | **Fresh K-marks.** A dozen trees in a loose circle around the party's current position have been freshly carved. They were not there when the party entered this area. No creature is visible. Listening carefully: something breathes. |
| 16 | **Dust wolves (1d3).** Wolves whose eyes are filmed white, moving in stuttering, arrhythmic patterns. They attack but their coordination is broken, all attack rolls made against them gain a +1 bonus. Morale is 7, reduced to 5 after the first of them takes damage. |
| 17 | **Old Brindle,** moving fast through the undergrowth, muttering. He will pause to speak briefly: *"The pool is thirsty tonight. Something fed it."* He then disappears into the dark before questions can be asked. He is heading toward Area 4. |
| 18 | **A survivor from the logging camp,** mute from shock, huddled under a fallen trunk. He cannot speak but can draw, given paper and charcoal, he will produce a crude but accurate map of the logging camp layout (Area 3) and indicate with frantic hashing where the beast came from. |
| 19 | **Dust trail.** A smeared path of glittering white powder and claw-prints leads north through the undergrowth. Following it requires no tracking rolls and leads directly to Area 5 (Thorn-Choked Hollow). The trail is fresh. The prints are still warm. |
| 20 | **The hollow is close.** The party finds a spray of powdered bone across the path, a claw-mark gouged eight feet up a pine, and the thick, cold smell of bitter sweetness. They have reached the threshold of Area 5 without further navigation. Proceed directly to the Thorn-Choked Hollow entry. |
