# The North Gate Event

*Run this scene when either 20 minutes of real time have elapsed in Ringstown OR the party has visited three or more locations, whichever comes first. It ends the town phase, delivers the commission, and propels the party into the Thicket with no reasonable alternative.*

---

## Setup

The event unfolds in three beats, each roughly a minute apart. The referee should not wait for a pause in player activity, interrupt freely. This is designed to cut through conversation.

---

## Beat One: The Gnome at the Gate

A commotion at the north gate. Warden Drev's voice, pitched high:

> "Someone get Elnara. Someone get Elnara *now.*"

A gnome berry-gatherer, one of the mountain gnomes, unmistakeable by size and the berry-stained fingers, has collapsed against the inside of the north gate. She is alive. Her left arm ends at the elbow, wrapped in a strip of her own coat. The wound is recent and cauterised, not cut, something burned it closed. She is holding a folded piece of bark in her remaining hand and will not let go of it.

If the party approaches before Elnara arrives, the gnome will fix whoever is nearest with absolute clarity and say:

> *"The hollow woke up three nights ago. Something is feeding it. You cannot wait."*

Then she loses consciousness.

**The bark map:** Anyone who pries it from her hand finds a crude but accurate sketch of the Thicket's interior, the ravine, the logging camp, the glade, and a hollow marked with a filled circle and the word *do not go alone* in Gnomish. A character who can read Gnomish or succeeds on an Intelligence check recognises it as a gnome trail map, updated recently. It grants a +1 bonus to any navigation or tracking rolls made in the Thicket.

---

## Beat Two: The Impact

Before anyone has finished processing the gnome, the north gate *moves.*

Not opens. Moves. The entire gate, six inches of iron-banded oak, shudders inward on its frame, throwing dust from the hinges. A sound follows: not a crash, but a sustained impact, like something very large pressing rather than striking. Then silence.

Then it happens again.

> The second impact is harder. A crack runs up the left gate post, hairline-thin but visible. The torches in the gatehouse go out. From the other side of the gate comes a sound that is not quite a roar, it is too rhythmic, too structured, and for just a moment it resolves into something that almost sounds like a repeated word.

The gate holds. The beast does not breach. After two impacts it stops, and the sounds of the Thicket, insects, wind, settling wood, return as though nothing happened.

The white powder sifts down from the gate frame like flour.

---

## Beat Three: Elnara's Commission

Elnara arrives at a run within thirty seconds of the second impact. She takes in the gnome, the cracked gate post, and the powder on the ground in one sweep, then turns to the party. She does not raise her voice.

> "I have been waiting three weeks for someone capable of going in there. The town is not safe while that thing is active and the dust is still at its source. I need it stopped, the beast, the pool, the cache, whatever is feeding this."
>
> "One hundred gold pieces, paid on your return with proof. Fifty more at minimum if you bring back anything that tells me who is behind the smuggling, and more depending on how much of the picture you find. I will have the gate open for you in five minutes."
>
> She glances at the gnome. "And if she told you anything useful, now would be the time to share it."

If the party attempts to negotiate, haggle, or stall, Elnara adds, without inflection:

> "The gate post cracked on the second hit. There will be a third."

---

## Referee Notes

**If the party still refuses to go:** The owlbear hits the gate a third time within the hour. The post fails. The gate can be barred but not latched. Elnara will make clear, still without raising her voice, that the town cannot hold against a sustained assault, and that the only way to stop the assaults is to deal with the source. This is not a threat; it is a material fact.

**On the gnome's timeline:** The gnome reports the hollow "woke up three nights ago." This corresponds to the owlbear's contamination spike and heightened pool activity. This heightened state lasts approximately 5–7 days from the adventure's start. A party that delays more than a week may find the frenzies less frequent and the pool's glow diminished — not resolved, but temporarily quieter. If a referee needs a mechanical guide: after 7 days, reduce the owlbear's Dust Frenzy uses to 2/day and the pool's ambient glow to barely visible. The gnome's warning is accurate about urgency, not about a hard deadline.

**On why Elnara trusts the party:** She arrived during or just after the second impact. Everyone who stayed at the gate rather than retreating into the square is already ahead of most. She does not explain this or put it into words — it is simply what she saw when she turned around. There is no audition because the gate just administered one.

**On the gnome:** She survives if given basic care. She will not accompany the party into the Thicket. She will confirm the bark map is accurate and add one detail: the hollow smells different at night than during the day. *"Sweeter. Like it is breathing."* She does not know why.

**On the powder at the gate:** A character who examines the powder sifted from the gate frame and has previously encountered white plume dust will recognise it immediately, the beast left residue on impact. The gate itself has been dusted. This confirms the owlbear is the source of spread, not merely a victim of it.

**On the bark map:** The map should be handed to the players as a physical prop if possible. It does not show Area 7 (the Riddle Cave), that location is not on gnome trail maps. Finding it is still an in-forest discovery.

**Timing note:** The entire event from Beat One to Elnara's commission should take no more than five minutes of table time. Keep descriptions short, keep Elnara's dialogue clipped, and do not invite extended discussion between beats. The pace of the scene is the point.
