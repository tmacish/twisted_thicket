# Calder Mourne
### Human Fighter — Your Background

---

You spent twelve years riding with the Shield Lands heavy cavalry. You were good at it. Then the regiment was dissolved in a political restructuring you have no interest in explaining to anyone, and you found yourself moderately experienced, moderately paid, and without a unit. That was four years ago.

Since then you have been taking independent security contracts. Escort work, clearance operations, the occasional thing that does not have an official name. You have built a reputation for following through, which is the only reputation in this line of work that actually pays.

You know **Ranger-Captain Elnara** from a previous job. You do not discuss the details of that job. What you know about her is: she is precise, she does not waste words, and she is not in the habit of exaggerating threats. When her letter arrived two weeks ago — *"Something in the Thicket needs ending. Your kind of work. Standard rates."* — you accepted without asking for details. You have learned that asking for details from Elnara is unproductive.

The cargo contract was simple enough. A gnome named **Blink Tinderflip** needed two coaches driven from Critwall to Ringstown, sensitive cargo, deliver intact to Elnara. You took the coachman role on one of them. You didn't ask what was inside. You never do.

---

### What Happened on the Road

Three days out of Critwall, half a mile from the Ringstown gate, something came out of the forest.

You got a look at it. You are a soldier. You have a considered professional opinion: it is large, it is fast, it is wrong in some way that you cannot fully articulate, and it needs to die. Whatever it hit, it hit hard enough to turn a coach into splinters and keep moving. You survived. Others may not have.

You are now in Ringstown. Elnara is at the gate. She did not look surprised.

---

### What You Know About the Region

The Twisted Thicket has a bad reputation among drovers. Three coaches have now been hit near its edge. Elnara mentioned it before you could.

You have heard of **White Plume Mountain** — most Shield Lands veterans have. It is north of Ringstown, it smokes constantly, it has a grim history. The drovers avoid the road that goes anywhere near it. You have no specific intelligence about what is happening there now, and you have not asked. You were hired to deal with what is in the Thicket, not what might be under a mountain.

---

### Who You Are

You do not have many words. You have objectives, progress checks, and outcomes. This is not coldness — you will follow a good plan from anyone regardless of rank or background, and you will stand between your party and whatever is trying to kill them, because that is the job.

You have watched soldiers make bad decisions under pressure for twelve years. You know what panic looks like, and you know what discipline looks like. You prefer the second.

You will not touch whatever powder the locals are apparently finding at the wreck sites. You have seen what happens to soldiers who supplement their courage chemically. The sequel is never worth the opening act.
