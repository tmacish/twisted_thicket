# Tasmin Reed
### Half-Elf Bard — Your Background

---

Your master died this past winter. Silvanius the Loremaster, bard, historian, man of considerable opinions about other people's songs, dead at last in a chair by a window with most of a sentence still in him. You were there. You held the pen until there was nothing left to write.

Twenty-four years with him. The first ten at his table, learning to read and write and transcribe before you had a fixed address. The last fourteen traveling and adventuring at his side, sitting with survivors, reading archives, and getting into the kind of trouble a loremaster and his apprentice reliably find when they go looking for true history. By the time he died, you had transcribed four major histories, traveled the length of the Shield Lands twice, sat through more archival correspondence than you will ever want to count, and learned more about the last hundred years of Oerth than most people learn in a lifetime. You know the names of the company that broke at Blackmoor and what they carried. You know about Aprif Al-Hazim, near-immortal, last seen descending into the Sunless Sea seven years ago and not returned. You know about the three weapons, the mountain, and what Silvanius spent the last decade of his life quietly afraid of.

You know more than he ever meant to teach you. You know he knew that.

---

### The Receipt

After the funeral, you sorted through his papers. It took three weeks. His correspondence alone filled six crates.

Near the bottom of the oldest crate, you found a merchant's receipt. Standard document, dated six weeks before the northern coaches started disappearing: transport of a warhammer matching Whelm's description, Ringstown to a waystation north of the Thicket. Someone had circled it in red ink. In the margin, in Silvanius's hand:

*not yet.*

You do not know what *not yet* means. You know what Whelm is. You know that Silvanius was afraid of the weapons reaching the mountain. You know that he circled this receipt and wrote those two words and put it at the bottom of a crate, and that he never told you about it.

You came north to find out what he knew that he didn't say.

---

### The Coach Contract

A gnome named **Blink Tinderflip** was hiring riders for a cargo run from Critwall to Ringstown. You took the bench of one of the coaches. The pay was adequate. More to the point, Ringstown was where you were going anyway.

You did not ask what was in the crates.

---

### What Happened on the Road

Something came out of the Thicket. You got a brief, clear look at it before the chaos started: owlbear-shaped, owlbear-sized, but luminescent. Pale light in the fur. Moving at a speed that was wrong.

You survived. You are at the Ringstown gate. **Ranger-Captain Elnara** is standing at the edge of the wreckage with an expression you have seen before, on scholars who have confirmed something they spent a long time hoping they were wrong about.

---

### What You Know About the Region

More than most people in this party, probably.

The Twisted Thicket sits north of Ringstown on the road toward White Plume Mountain. The mountain has been smoking year-round for as long of records exist. A wizard named Keraptis was associated with it, took three legendary weapons from their owners years ago, and disappeared. The weapons were Wave, Whelm, and Blackrazor. Silvanius believed the three weapons were not stolen. He believed they were moved deliberately, and that bringing them back together at the mountain was the one thing that must not happen. He wrote this down and then crossed it out and then wrote it again in a different place. He never explained why.

White plume dust — you read his notes on it — is a byproduct of research conducted at or near the mountain. Exposure produces compulsive behavior: carving a specific letter. The letter is K.

You have not handled the dust. You understand, from his notes, what it does to the mind under prolonged exposure, and you have no interest in finding out firsthand.

---

### Your Magic

The bardic colleges Silvanius trained under, and trained you from, follow an older tradition. The bards common since the war learn their spells from arcane formularies, the same root as a wizard's. Silvanius had opinions about this. You heard them many times.

Your magic comes from a different channel. It runs through druidic practice: nature's patterns, the world's own attention, the deep logic of living things. Detect Magic, in your hands, is not a formula imposed on the world. It is listening. Faerie Fire is the light the deep forest makes on its own, borrowed briefly and given direction.

You cannot cast wizard spells. A scroll with an arcane formula will work once if you read it aloud, but it will not feel like yours. Silvanius called the arcane approach "faster to learn and faster to exhaust" and did not mean it as a compliment.

He meant you were slower to impress, harder to surprise, and better suited for the long work. He was, as usual, annoyingly correct.

---

### Who You Are

You take notes on everything. Two journals since arriving in Ringstown. You started a third this morning.

You are a working bard, which means you know how to talk to people, how to listen, how to read a room. You are not a fighter. You will contribute to a fight if asked, and you carry a sword that belonged to a man who knew what he was doing with it. Whether that transfers is an open question.

You know things about the history of this place that the people around you do not know you know. You have not decided yet how much of it to share, or when. Silvanius had a rule: information given too early saves no one; information given too late saves no one; information given correctly changes things. He was annoyingly right about this, as he was about most things.

Your father died when you were too young to remember him. You have spent your life watching someone else remember him for you. You are, on balance, at peace with this.

Probably.
