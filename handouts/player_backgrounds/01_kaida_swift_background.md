# Kaida Swift
### Human Thief — Your Background

---

Six years in the Critwall information markets has taught you that the people who ask the right questions early get paid; the people who ask the wrong questions late get hurt. You do courier work, evidence recovery, the occasional quiet extraction. You are good at not being seen, good at finding things people have hidden, and very good at knowing when a job has more layers than the client admitted to.

This job has layers.

---

### The Visible Contract

You were hired to ride along with two coaches carrying sealed cargo from Critwall to **Ringstown**, delivering to a woman named **Ranger-Captain Elnara**. The gnome who arranged it — **Blink Tinderflip**, cheerful, evasive, and already gone by the time the coaches left — called the cargo "sensitive." The pay was better than a simple escort warrants. You took the job and kept your questions to yourself, which is a professional habit.

---

### The Other Contract

You have a second client. A Critwall merchant factor you have worked for before, a cautious man who became very interested in a particular trade substance after one of his associates died from exposure to it. The substance is called *plume's kiss* in certain circles, or *white plume dust* in less careful conversation. Your client wants names, a supply chain map, and documentation. He is not asking you to destroy anything. He wants to know who is moving it and where it originates.

You have not shared this with anyone else on the coaches. Whether you share it with the people you end up working alongside is your call. Information is a resource, and resources are not distributed until there is a reason to distribute them.

---

### What Happened on the Road

Something came out of the Thicket and put one of the coaches into splinters. You survived. You are not entirely clear on how. The creature was moving at a speed that should not have been possible for something that large, and where its hide caught the torchlight it threw off cold white sparks.

You are now in Ringstown. The gate is open. A woman in ranger's leathers is standing there looking unsurprised, which tells you something about the situation you have walked into.

---

### What You Know About the Region

White powder. That's the thread. You knew before you arrived that something called white plume dust was being moved through this area. You've heard it comes from somewhere north — *north* in the direction of a mountain that smokes year-round and has a reputation for killing the people who go near it. You don't have anything more specific than that. Yet.

The people in Ringstown who know anything about the dust are not talking freely. That's usually a sign that they're either afraid or paid. You intend to find out which.

---

### Who You Are

You watch hands. It is an occupational habit. You are not specifically suspicious of any one person — you are equally suspicious of everyone, which is a more useful baseline.

You share information when it is tactically useful to do so. You don't consider this secretive. You consider it honest: you are a professional, and professionals manage their information deliberately.

One more thing: whatever the dust is and wherever it comes from, you do not intend to try it. Your client's associate died from it. You've read the description of what it does on the way down, and you've noted that "compulsively carving a letter into surfaces" is not on your list of acceptable occupational hazards.
