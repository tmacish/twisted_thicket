# Sable Crane
### Human Ranger — Your Background

---

You worked game warden patrol in the roadwoods south of the Thicket for four years before the garrison was cut and you were let go with back pay and a handshake. You have been working the same routes freelance ever since — leading trapper parties, scouting for merchant groups, the occasional boundary survey. You know the Thicket's southern approaches better than anyone currently alive in Ringstown who is not missing.

You knew something was wrong before anyone official acknowledged it. The birds told you. Or rather: the birds stopped telling you things, which is how the forest tells you that an area has gone bad.

---

### What You Observed

Three weeks ago the songbirds stopped at the treeline. Not fewer birds — no birds. The silence has an edge to it that you have learned to read.

Two weeks ago the deer stopped coming to the southern meadows. The game trails into the Thicket's edge went cold.

One week ago you found a dead fox at the treeline with white powder on its muzzle. You brought the carcass to **Ranger-Captain Elnara** at the Ringstown gatehouse. She looked at it and said: *"I know."*

That response — two words, no explanation, no follow-up — is the reason you decided you needed to be on whatever team went in.

You went back to Elnara. You told her you knew the southern approaches, the water sources, the ridge lines, and where the three-toed prints had been appearing on the game trails before the animals stopped using them. She gave you a contract and told you to be at the north gate.

---

### The Coach Contract

You came to Ringstown via the main road from Critwall, which is how you ended up riding on one of two coaches carrying sealed cargo for a gnome named **Blink Tinderflip**. The pay was reasonable and the direction was right. You didn't ask what the crates held.

---

### What Happened on the Road

You know the three-toed prints from the game trails. When something with those prints came out of the treeline and took one of the coaches apart in four strides, you had a long moment of professional recalibration. You had seen the prints. You had measured the stride. You had not fully accounted for what that stride means at speed.

You survived. The owlbear — if it is still an owlbear in any meaningful sense — went back into the trees.

You are now at the Ringstown gate. You have a considered opinion about what you just saw, and that opinion is: this is worse than you told anyone it was, and you told Elnara it was bad.

---

### What You Know About the Region

You know the Thicket the way you know a person you've worked with for years — its moods, its usual sounds, the things that feel wrong before you can name them. What is happening in there now is not natural. Animals don't abandon territory like this. The contamination is spreading outward from somewhere in the northern interior, and it has been spreading for at least a season.

**White Plume Mountain** is north. You can see the smoke from the high meadows on a clear day. Drovers and trappers give it distance and don't discuss it in detail. The old-timers call it cursed ground. You have no specific intelligence about what is there, but you know what direction things are getting worse from.

---

### Who You Are

You talk to yourself in the forest. You have been doing it for years. You do not realise you do it.

You are patient. You will wait longer than anyone else before concluding something is gone or not coming back. This is a virtue in the field and occasionally a problem elsewhere.

The owlbear you saw cross the southern meadow six weeks ago was moving normally. It was a healthy animal. You know what it was before it became whatever it is now, and that matters to you in a way you are not entirely comfortable with.
