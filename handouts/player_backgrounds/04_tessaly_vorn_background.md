# Tessaly Vorn
### Human Cleric (St. Cuthbert) — Your Background

---

Your temple sent you north because three separate reports arrived in the same month describing the same thing: survivors who compulsively carved a specific letter — *K* — into whatever surface was nearest to them. Walls, floors, skin. They did not remember doing it. Some of them were still doing it when the reports were written.

The letter is not a holy symbol. It is not associated with any deity your temple recognises. And yet the compulsion it produces is eerily similar to certain ritual states that your order has documented across two centuries of theological study. The similarity is what alarmed the senior curates. The dissimilarity — the fact that no known god answers to that letter — is what alarmed them more.

Your temple's assessment was this: either something new is making plays through a physical substance and calling itself by an old wizard's initial, or something very old has found a new method. Either possibility required investigation on the ground. You were the available curate with relevant field experience. You packed the Teachings and left within the week.

---

### What You Found in Ringstown

You have been here one week. You spent most of it in **Brother Aldric's** infirmary, reviewing his case notes. He has seventeen documented patients with dust exposure symptoms. He is a careful man, overwhelmed, and visibly relieved to have a theological context for what his patients are experiencing. You have been providing that context.

Your working conclusion: the substance is not divine in origin. It does not carry the signature of genuine divine compulsion. Whatever it is, it is a counterfeit — sophisticated, but counterfeit. You find this both reassuring and, in a different way, more troubling. Counterfeits imply someone who studied the original.

When the opportunity arose to join an expedition into the Thicket, you joined it. You are not finished investigating.

---

### The Coach Contract

You were at the Ringstown gate when the coaches from Critwall were expected. You ended up riding on one of them for the last stretch of road, a practical arrangement. The cargo was sealed. You were told it was sensitive and handled with care. You did not press for details — that is Elnara's business, and Elnara does not invite follow-up questions.

---

### What Happened on the Road

The beast came out of the forest before you reached the gate. You have now seen it at close range. You noted: it is very large, it moves at an impossible speed, and where its hide caught the torchlight it threw off sparks that were cold and white — the same colour as the powder you have been documenting.

You made a note. You are concerned that you are still in a note-taking state of mind when you probably should have been in a different state of mind.

You survived. You are now at the gate. Elnara is here. She said: *"That's the third carriage this tenday."*

---

### What You Know About the Region

The compulsion the dust produces focuses on a single letter: *K*. In Ringstown, people whisper about a dead wizard — **Keraptis** — who supposedly had something to do with the mountain north of here. You have read about Keraptis in theological archives, the kind of footnote that appears in chapters about magical hubris and its consequences. He has been dead for well over a century.

Whether someone is using his name, or whether something connected to him is genuinely active, is the question you came here to answer. You do not yet have the answer.

**White Plume Mountain** smokes year-round. You can see it from the north gate. The smoke is heavy. You have begun to think the smoke and the dust are connected, and you have begun to think the connection is not metaphorical.

---

### Who You Are

You take notes on everything. Your journal has twelve pages on dust symptom progression, four pages on theological parallels, and a sketch of the K-mark that you notice yourself redrawing when you are thinking about something else.

St. Cuthbert is a god of honesty and righteous retribution. You apply both: you will tell the people you work with the truth about your objectives, and you will not leave an evil that can be ended, unended.

You have administered last rites three times since arriving in Ringstown. You are tired in a way you are not going to mention.
