# Wulfric Stone
### Dwarf Fighter — Your Background

---

Fifteen years garrison work in the Shield Lands. Eventually made sergeant-equivalent. Then a border reduction dissolved the unit and left you moderately well-paid and without a wall to watch. You have been doing security and escort work with **Calder Mourne** on and off for three years — he finds the jobs, you pack the plate mail, nobody discusses it as a formal arrangement because it does not need to be formal.

When Calder said *"Elnara has a job in the north, involving a large and dangerous animal,"* you said *"Reasonable"* and started packing. You did not ask for additional details, because in your experience additional details rarely change what you end up doing with your hammer.

---

### The Coach Contract

You and Calder signed on as outriders and coachmen for a cargo run from Critwall to Ringstown, hired by a gnome named **Blink Tinderflip**. Two coaches, sealed cargo, deliver intact to **Ranger-Captain Elnara** at the Ringstown gatehouse. The pay was better than escort work usually pays, which you noted without drawing particular conclusions. The crates were sealed. You did not ask what was inside.

---

### What Happened on the Road

Three days in. Half a mile from the gate. Something came out of the forest.

You have been in garrison work for fifteen years. You have a professional, measured opinion about threats. Your professional, measured opinion about what came out of that forest is: it was very large, it was very fast, it was luminescent in a way that no animal you have encountered has been luminescent, and the coach it hit ceased to exist as a coach and became wreckage in approximately four strides.

You survived. So did Calder, as far as you can tell. You are now at the Ringstown gate. Elnara is standing there looking at the wreckage with an expression that you would describe as recognition rather than surprise.

You have a considered professional opinion about the animal and what needs to happen to it. Calder likely shares it.

---

### What You Know About the Region

Almost nothing specific, which is usually where you start. You know the Thicket has a bad reputation — drovers have been refusing this road for weeks. You know Ringstown is a frontier post on the Shield Lands edge. You know there is a mountain north of here that smokes year-round; you can see it from the gate.

**White Plume Mountain.** Dwarves do not, as a rule, have warm feelings about mountains they did not build in. That one you have heard mentioned in a tone that makes experienced garrison soldiers speak more quietly than usual. You don't know the specifics. You know it's north and that nobody goes there if they can avoid it.

You do not have opinions about ancient wizards, mountain politics, or whatever history might be attached to the smoke up there. You have opinions about whether something is trying to kill you and what to do about it. These opinions are usually correct and sometimes sufficient.

---

### One Unusual Thing

Since the coach wreck, there has been something. A sensation you do not have a word for. A low vibration, almost below hearing, like a hammer struck on a stone floor far below and you are feeling it through the soles of your boots. It comes and goes.

You have been in stone buildings, underground tunnels, and mine workings. You know what it feels like when something is resonating below you. This is not that, exactly. It is more directional. It seems to be coming from the northeast.

You have not mentioned this to anyone. You are not certain it is real. But you are a dwarf, and dwarves are not given to imagining vibrations that aren't there.

---

### Who You Are

You do not elaborate. You state facts, ask one question when you need to, and proceed. People have described this as brusque. You consider brusque to be neutral.

You are loyal to people you have worked with. Calder is in that category. You would not say this out loud to anyone, including Calder. It doesn't need to be said.

You will not touch the dust. Not from moral principle — from practical observation. You have watched soldiers supplement their courage with questionable substances throughout your career. The sequel to that story is never worth the opening act.
