A shield of gold through the darkest of gales,
The champion Jeggs, in his shimmering scales.
With a hand held in friendship for pauper and king,
He sought out the light that the heavens might bring.
No shadow could linger, no spirit would cease,
Till the heart of the wanderer finally found peace.

Though the fires of the mountain he never did tread,
He stood where the marshes of Black Moor ran red.
A bulwark of valor when the heavens fell dark,
Leaving on history a radiant mark.
But the clash of the iron and the trumpet's long cry,
Are traded for waves and a turquoise sky.

The weight of his armor was cast to the sand,
As a welcoming warmth reached for his hand.
No more shall he bleed for the sake of the crown,
In the gardens of Elves, he has laid his sword down.
The silver-leafed forests and shimmering miles,
Of the hallowed and hidden Lendore Isles.

Beneath the blue moon and the eternal bough,
The lines of the conflict are erased from his brow.
For the spirit of Jeggs is a beacon that glows,
In the land of the sunset, where quietude flows.
A paladin's rest, earned in grace and in gold,
In the peace of the Isles, his story is told.

Signed,

Silvanius
