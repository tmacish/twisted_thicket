Through sulfurous clouds and the mountain's hot breath,
Four legends descended to challenge a death.
The witch Ivy wove through the shimmering haze,
While Tsich’lli, the peerless, led through the maze.
With a blade like a comet and spells of old vine,
They marched where the embers and shadows entwine.

Above them, the wings of the great ones took flight,
Moonmist, a glimmer of pale lunar light,
And Ebonfrost, carved from the glacier’s dark heart,
Tearing the veil of the chaos apart.
Together they shielded the world from the flame,
Writing in fire a permanent name.

Into the maw of the White Plume they went,
Their strength and their magic and spirit all spent.
The earth gave a shudder, the sky turned to gray,
As the darkness that threatened was driven away.
The world was delivered, the peril was slain,
By the witch and the warrior and dragons’ refrain.

But the mountain fell silent, the cavern doors closed,
Where the heroes of old in the silence reposed.
No scale from the dragons, no glint from the sword,
Returned from the depths to the light they restored.
They remain in the stone where the white plumes arise,
The guardians lost under ash-covered skies.
