# Aldwyn Corr, Human Paladin 6

*Knight-errant of the Order of the Radiant Shield, a holy order sworn to Heironeous operating out of the central Shield Lands. He was dispatched north when the Order received reports of a substance producing compulsive behaviour around a specific divine name. Heironeous does not share names with dead wizards. Aldwyn is here to establish the distinction.*

## Paladin Abilities

**Detect Evil:** At will, within 60 ft. Requires concentration for one round. Reveals the presence and approximate direction of evil intent, evil creatures, and evil-aligned magical objects. Does not reveal exact location or nature. Structured, deliberate evil reads differently than passionate malice — Aldwyn finds the former more unsettling.

**Lay on Hands:** Once per day, Aldwyn may heal by touch. Heals 2 hp per paladin level, currently **12 hp total per day**, divided among as many recipients and as many touches as he chooses. He cannot use this on himself.

**Aura of Protection:** All evil creatures within 10 ft of Aldwyn suffer −1 to attack rolls against any target. All companions within 10 ft receive +1 to saving throws against magic cast by evil sources. This aura is always active and requires no action.

**Immune to Disease:** Aldwyn cannot contract natural or magical disease.

**Immune to Fear:** Aldwyn is immune to fear effects, magical or mundane.

**Cure Disease:** Once per week, Aldwyn may cure any disease in a creature he touches. This can alleviate physical compulsion symptoms; it does not extract a substance from the body, but it can clear behavioural overrides over the course of one turn.

**Turn Undead:** As a Cleric of level 2 (four levels below paladin level). Rarely relevant in this adventure.

**Saving Throw Bonus:** +2 to all saves (already reflected in the table above).

## Magic Items

**Plate Mail +1:** Ceremonial armour of the Order, not ornate, but well-maintained and faintly engraved with Heironeous's lightning bolt at the shoulder. Provides AC 1 combined with his shield. The enchantment is old; the armour was worn by a previous knight of the Order who died on a mission Aldwyn's commander described as "instructive."

**Longsword +1, Holy:** The sword was blessed by a senior priest of Heironeous before Aldwyn's departure. Against undead and chaotic evil creatures, it deals an additional 1d6 damage per hit. Against all other targets it functions as a standard +1 weapon. The blade does not glow or make noise. Holiness, in Aldwyn's understanding, is not theatrical.

**Potion of Heroism:** A deep crimson liquid that temporarily improves the imbiber's combat abilities for 2d6+10 rounds. Grants the equivalent of 2 additional fighter levels, improving THAC0 by 2 and adding 2d10 temporary hit points. Aldwyn received this from his Order's armoury. He has not used it. He is waiting for something that requires it.

## Background

Aldwyn has been with the Order of the Radiant Shield for eleven years, having entered as a squire at fourteen. He was posted to a border garrison for four years, then spent three years as a travelling knight-errant dealing with isolated threats across the Shield Lands interior, then two years assigned to the Order's intelligence function, not espionage exactly, but the quiet work of following up on reports that come in from remote parishes about things that smell like divine interference but are not. The white plume dust reports fit that category precisely. His superior sent him north with a simple instruction: *"Find out if Keraptis is involved. If he is, find out how. If he isn't, find out what is using his name and why."*

## Personality

- Formal without being cold. Addresses people by name when he knows it and by role when he doesn't. Has been told this seems distant. Considers clarity to be a form of respect.
- Takes the dust's compulsion effects more seriously than almost anyone else in the party, not because he is afraid, but because he has theological training in the difference between genuine divine compulsion and its counterfeits, and he finds the counterfeit quality of the dust deeply offensive.
- Will not execute a subdued enemy without due cause. Will stand between the party and someone they are about to kill in anger. Has done this before. Has the scars to prove it worked out eventually.
