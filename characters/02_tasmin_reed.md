## Bard Abilities

**Legend Lore (30%):** Tasmin may attempt to recall specific historical information about a legendary item, notable figure, or significant location.

**Fascinate:** Once per encounter, Tasmin may attempt to fascinate a creature within 30 ft. through song or performance. The target must save vs. spells or stand transfixed for 1d4 rounds. Breaks immediately if the target is physically threatened or harmed. Unintelligent creatures and creatures under magical compulsion are immune.

**Counter Song:** While performing, Tasmin can attempt to negate sound-based magical effects. Allies within 30 ft. receive +2 to saving throws against charm, fear, and compulsion effects delivered through sound.

**Druidic Spell Tradition:** Tasmin was trained in the old bardic college form, which draws from the druidic tradition rather than arcane study. Her spells are drawn from the druid list, not the magic-user list. She cannot cast wizard spells. A magic-user scroll read aloud will function once, but it will not feel like hers.

## Background

Tasmin's father was a priest of Heironeous named Lyrion. He died before she was three. The story she was raised on was that a purple worm swallowed him whole in the Temple of Death and, finding him not to its liking, returned his holy symbol and very little else. Silvanius sang at the funeral. She has heard the song. It is three verses. Her father would have wanted forty.

She grew up as Silvanius's apprentice from the day of that funeral, twenty-four years in total. The first ten she spent learning at his table, reading every letter twice and writing down everything he said before she had a fixed address. The last fourteen she traveled and adventured with him across the Shield Lands, sitting with survivors, reading archives, and getting into the kind of trouble a loremaster and his apprentice reliably find when they go looking for true history. He taught her history the way he practiced it: by talking to every person who had been there, reading every letter twice, and refusing to write down anything as true unless at least two people who remembered it agreed.

She was with him when he died. She has his notes, his undelivered letters from Aprif, and his last unfinished sentence.

She came north because in sorting through those papers she found something she could not explain: a merchant's receipt dated six weeks before the coaches began disappearing from the northern road, for transport of a warhammer matching Whelm's description through Ringstown toward the Thicket. Silvanius had circled it in red ink. He had written two words next to it: *not yet.*

She does not know what *not yet* means. She came north to find out.

## Personality

- Takes notes on everything. Has filled two journals since arriving in Ringstown. Has started a third.
- Knows more about the history of the three weapons and the Battle of Blackmoor than anyone she has met, including people who were there and have since tried to forget it. She does not lead with this.
- Her father died before she knew him. She grew up watching Silvanius eulogize him once a year with decreasing embellishment and the same three jokes about the purple worm. She considers herself at peace with this. On balance this is probably true.
